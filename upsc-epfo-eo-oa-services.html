Enforcement Officer (EO)

Primary Role:

Ensure compliance with EPF & MP Act, 1952. Conduct inspections, inquiries, recovery proceedings, and settle claims.

Key Responsibilities:

Field inspections, compliance verification, claim settlement, awareness programs, recovery actions.

Example: An EO might inspect a factory in Gurgaon to verify PF compliance for 500 workers.

Accounts Officer (AO)

Primary Role:

Maintain accounts, manage funds, prepare financial statements, audit accounts, and ensure financial compliance.

Key Responsibilities:

Account maintenance, financial reporting, auditing, cash management, budget preparation.

Example: An AO might prepare the annual financial statement for EPFO's Bangalore region.

Recruitment Test Syllabus

Objective Type | Negative Marking | 120 Questions

Complete Syllabus:

  • General English: Comprehension, Grammar, Vocabulary
  • Indian Freedom Struggle: Major events, leaders, movements
  • Current Events & Developmental Issues: National & international importance
  • Indian Polity & Economy: Constitution, governance, economic development
  • General Accounting Principles: Basic accounting concepts
  • Industrial Relations & Labour Laws: EPF Act, Industrial Disputes Act, Factories Act
  • General Science & Computer Applications: Basic science, computer fundamentals
  • General Mental Ability & Quantitative Aptitude: Reasoning, arithmetic
  • Social Security in India: EPFO schemes, pension, insurance

Labour Laws and Social Security carry maximum weightage (approx 30-40%).

EPFO Officer Career Path

1. Enforcement Officer / Accounts Officer

Entry Level. Group B Gazetted Officer. Field/Office responsibilities.

2. Assistant PF Commissioner (5-8 Years)

First promotion. Supervisory role, managing teams and larger jurisdictions.

3. Regional PF Commissioner (10-15 Years)

Regional Head. Overseeing multiple offices, policy implementation.

4. Additional Central PF Commissioner

National level leadership. Key policymaking and strategic planning.

5. Central PF Commissioner

Highest post in EPFO. Overall administration of organization.

Promotions based on performance, departmental exams, and seniority.
