Grade C vs Grade D

üèÜ Stenographer Grade 'C'

Description

This is a Group 'B' Non-Gazetted post. It involves higher responsibility and works in top Ministries.

Locations

Mostly located in Delhi (Ministries/Headquarters).

Pay Scale

Level 6 (GP 4200) or Level 7 (GP 4600). Higher salary.

üíº Stenographer Grade 'D'

Description

This is a Group 'C' post. It is an entry-level stenography role in subordinate offices.

Locations

Can be posted anywhere in India (All India Liability).

Pay Scale

Level 4 (GP 2400). Standard entry-level government pay.

Computer Based Exam

Objective Type (MCQ). 200 Marks.

  • Mode: Online (Computer Based).
  • Type: Multiple Choice Questions.
  • Sections:
    • General Intelligence & Reasoning (50 Qs)
    • General Awareness (50 Qs)
    • English Language & Comprehension (100 Qs)
  • Time: 2 Hours (120 Minutes).
  • Negative Marking: 0.25 marks deducted for every wrong answer.

Skill Test (Shorthand)

Qualifying in nature.

The Process

1. You will be given a dictation for 10 minutes.
2. You must write it in Shorthand.
3. Then transcribe it on a computer.

Transcription Time

  • Grade D (80 wpm): 50 mins (English), 65 mins (Hindi).
  • Grade C (100 wpm): 40 mins (English), 55 mins (Hindi).

Career Progression

From Grade D to Gazetted Officer.

1. Stenographer Grade 'D'

Entry Level. Taking dictation, typing, clerical work.

2. Stenographer Grade 'C'

First Promotion. Higher pay, working with senior officers.

3. Private Secretary (PS)

Gazetted Post. Handling confidential matters for officers.

4. Senior Private Secretary (SPS)

Highly respected role. Working with Joint Secretaries/Directors.

5. Principal Private Secretary (PPS)

Top level administrative support.
