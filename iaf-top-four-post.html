Eligibility criteria vary for each entry scheme, but some common requirements include:

  • üéì
    Education: Varies from **10+2 (with Physics & Maths)** for NDA to a **Graduate/Engineering Degree** for AFCAT, CDS, and other entries.
  • üéÇ

  • üáÆüá≥
    Nationality & Gender: Must be a citizen of India. Both male and female candidates are eligible for most branches.

Physical & Medical Standards üìä

Candidates must be physically and medically fit as per IAF standards.

  • Flying Branch: Stringent requirements for height (min. 162.5 cms), leg length, and eyesight (6/6 in one eye and 6/9 in other, correctable to 6/6).
  • Technical/Ground Duty: Different standards for height and weight apply.
  • General Health: Must be free of any disease/disability. Tattoos are subject to specific IAF policies.

5. Training at AFA, Dundigal ‚úàÔ∏è

Selected candidates, known as Flight Cadets, undergo rigorous training at the prestigious Air Force Academy (AFA) in Dundigal, near Hyderabad, to be molded into future military leaders.

1. The Curriculum

Training is a blend of physical conditioning, military tactics, academics, and specialized branch training.

  • Joint training for all branches in initial stages.
  • Flying training on simulators and basic trainer aircraft.
  • Technical training on aeronautical engineering.
  • Ground Duty training in administration, logistics, accounts etc.

2. Key Objectives

The goal is to develop cadets into officers who are leaders of men, possess a warrior ethos, and are professionally competent.

  • Inculcate discipline, integrity, and a sense of duty.
  • Impart knowledge of air power and its application.
  • Develop physical and mental toughness.
  • Foster camaraderie and teamwork.

6. IAF Officer Branches üåü

The IAF is broadly divided into three branches, each offering a unique and challenging career path.

Flying Branch

  • Fighter Pilot
  • Helicopter Pilot
  • Transport Pilot
  • Navigator

Technical Branch

  • Aeronautical Engineer (Mechanical)
  • Aeronautical Engineer (Electronics)

Ground Duty Branch

  • Administration & Logistics
  • Accounts & Education
  • Air Traffic Control (ATC)
  • Meteorology

üí° Why is this important?

Your chosen branch determines your role in the IAF's mission, from active combat roles in the cockpit to ensuring the service runs efficiently on the ground.

8. Your Preparation Strategy: Aim High üìà

Success requires a two-pronged strategy: diligent preparation for the written exam and holistic personality development for the AFSB interview.

üìö Key Steps:

  1. Master the Written Syllabus

    Thoroughly prepare for your chosen exam (AFCAT/NDA/CDS). Focus on quantitative aptitude, reasoning, English, and general awareness.

  2. Develop Physical Fitness

    Engage in regular physical activities like running, skipping, and sports to build the stamina required for the demanding military training.

  3. Enhance General Awareness

    Read newspapers daily. Stay informed about national/international affairs, defence news, and aviation-related developments.

üîÑ Habits for AFSB Success:

  • Be Authentic: The AFSB is a test of personality. Be yourself; do not try to project a fake persona.
  • Improve Communication Skills: Practice expressing your thoughts clearly and logically. Participate in group discussions and public speaking.
  • Introspect: Understand your own strengths, weaknesses, and life experiences. This is crucial for psychological tests and the interview.
  • Live an Active Life: Take initiative in college, at home, and in sports. This builds leadership qualities and a well-rounded personality.

Written Examination

This is the first hurdle. For most entries (AFCAT, NDA, CDS), you must clear a competitive written exam. It tests your knowledge in subjects like English, Maths, General Knowledge, and Reasoning. Clearing the cutoff is mandatory to receive a call for the AFSB interview.

AFSB (Air Force Selection Board) Interview

The AFSB is a 5-day comprehensive personality assessment. It includes psychological tests, group tasks, and a personal interview to determine your suitability as a potential officer. For flying branch candidates, it also includes the Computerised Pilot Selection System (CPSS) test.

Medical Examination & Final Merit

Candidates recommended by the AFSB undergo a thorough medical examination at designated Air Force medical centers. A final All-India Merit List is prepared based on the combined score of the written test and AFSB interview. Candidates who make the merit list are issued joining letters.
