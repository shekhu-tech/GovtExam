6. Officer Streams at SEBI üìä

SEBI recruits officers across various specialized streams to meet its diverse functional needs. Your stream will define your initial role and career progression within the organization.

General Stream

  • Largest number of vacancies.
  • Covers a wide range of SEBI functions.
  • Roles in departments like Surveillance, Enforcement, etc.

Legal Stream

  • Handles legal matters, litigation.
  • Drafts rules, regulations, and orders.
  • Requires a Law Degree.

IT Stream

  • Manages SEBI's IT infrastructure.
  • Develops and maintains systems.
  • Focus on cybersecurity, data analysis.

Research Stream

  • Conducts economic research.
  • Provides data-driven insights for policy.
  • Focus on market analysis, risk modeling.

Official Language

  • Handles official language-related work.
  • Translation and implementation of Hindi as official language.

üí° Why is this important?

The stream you choose at the time of application determines your eligibility and the syllabus for your Phase I and II Paper 2 exams. Choose the stream that aligns with your educational background and career goals.

8. Your Preparation Strategy: Smart Work üìà

Success in the SEBI Grade 'B' exam requires a planned and disciplined approach. Focus on a comprehensive strategy that covers all stages of the examination.

üìö Key Steps:

  1. Understand the Syllabus

  2. Current Affairs & Economy

    Focus on business and financial news. Read a good financial newspaper daily and stay updated on government schemes, policies, and economic indicators.

  3. Practice and Mock Tests

    Regularly solve previous year's question papers and attempt mock tests. This helps in time management and familiarizes you with the exam environment.

  • Interview Preparation: Start early with reading about SEBI's functions, its history, and recent regulatory changes.

Phase I Exam

This is an online screening test consisting of two papers. Paper 1 is common for all streams, while Paper 2 is specific to the stream you have applied for (e.g., General, Legal, IT, etc.). The marks from Phase I are for shortlisting only and are not added to the final score.

Phase II Exam

This is an online mains examination with two papers. Paper 1 is a descriptive English test, and Paper 2 is a multiple-choice paper on a specialized subject related to your chosen stream. The marks from Phase II are considered for the final merit list.

Final Interview

Candidates who clear the Phase II cut-off are called for a personal interview. The interview assesses your overall suitability for the role, including your communication skills, personality, and depth of knowledge. The final merit list is prepared based on the marks from Phase II and the interview.
