5. Training & Probation ЁЯПЫя╕П

New appointees are confirmed in service after successfully completing a mandatory probation period, during which they receive a fixed pay.

1. Fixed Pay & Probation

Initial appointment is on a fixed-pay basis.

  • The initial period (often 5 years for Class-III) is on a fixed salary.
  • The first two years of this period are considered as probation.
  • Performance is evaluated for confirmation and eligibility for a regular pay scale.

2. On-the-Job Training

Focuses on departmental procedures and rules.

  • Induction on Gujarat state service rules and conduct.
  • Practical, on-the-job training related to the specific duties of the post.

6. Key Responsibilities ЁЯМЯ

These roles are diverse and fundamental to the delivery of public services across the districts of Gujarat.

Admin & Revenue

  • **Clerk/DEO:** Administrative duties in Collectorates.
  • **Talati (Patwari):** Land record and revenue management at the village level.
  • **Court Staff:** Supporting the district judiciary.

Public Welfare

  • **Health Dept:** ANM, GNM in health centers.
  • **Education Dept:** Guest Teachers (Vidya Sahayak), BRPs.
  • **ICDS:** Anganwadi staff for child and maternal care.

Local Governance

  • **Panchayat Sahayak:** Assisting in Gram Panchayat work.
  • **Municipal Staff:** Providing urban civic amenities.
  • **DRDA Staff:** Implementing rural development programs.

Notification on OJAS

Timeline: Vacancies are advertised on the Online Job Application System (OJAS) portal of the Gujarat government.

Timeline: A written test is conducted a few months after the application process closes.

Timeline: Shortlisted candidates are called for computer proficiency tests and certificate verification.

Final Appointment

Timeline: A final merit list is prepared, and appointment letters are issued by the respective departments.
