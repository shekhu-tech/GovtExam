Step 1: PFT & PMT

The Physical Fitness Test (PFT) is the first hurdle. It includes a 1.6 km run, pull-ups, a 9-feet ditch jump, and a zig-zag balance test. This is followed by the Physical Measurement Test (PMT) for height, weight, and chest. This is a qualifying stage with marks awarded for the run and pull-ups.

Step 2: Medical Test

Candidates who clear the PFT and PMT undergo a detailed medical examination by Army doctors to ensure they are free from any disease or disability and meet the stringent medical standards required for military service.

Step 3: Written Exam (CEE)

Medically fit candidates appear for the Common Entrance Examination (CEE). It is an objective-type exam testing basic knowledge of GK, Science, Maths, and Reasoning. The marks from this exam are crucial for the final merit list.

Final Selection & Training

A final merit list is prepared based on the total marks from the PFT and CEE. Candidates are selected in order of merit. Those selected are dispatched to various regimental training centers for Basic Military Training (BMT).

Step 4: Final Merit List

The final merit list is prepared based on the aggregate marks obtained in the Physical Fitness Test (PFT) and the Common Entrance Examination (CEE). Candidates who rank high enough in the merit list are selected for enlistment.

Infantry (Frontline Combat) ü§∫

As an infantry soldier, you are the first line of defense. Your duties include patrolling borders, counter-insurgency operations, and engaging the enemy in direct combat.

Example: You could be part of a patrol team in Jammu & Kashmir, responsible for preventing infiltration and maintaining security in the area.

Artillery (Gun Crews) üí£

In the Artillery, you would be part of a team that operates large-caliber guns, mortars, and rocket launchers to provide fire support to the infantry during battle.

Example: During a conflict, you would be responsible for loading, aiming, and firing the Bofors or Dhanush howitzers to neutralize enemy positions from a distance.

Armoured Corps (Tank Crew) üõ°Ô∏è

As part of a tank crew (driver, gunner, or loader), you operate and maintain battle tanks like the T-90 or Arjun, spearheading attacks and providing shock action.

Example: In the plains of Punjab or Rajasthan, you would be training as a gunner, practicing hitting targets with the tank's main gun during firing exercises.

Support Roles üõ†Ô∏è

GD soldiers also serve in support arms like the Corps of Engineers (building bridges, clearing minefields) or the Army Service Corps (managing supplies and transport).

Example: As a soldier in the Corps of Engineers, you might be tasked with constructing a temporary bridge over a river to allow troops and vehicles to cross during an operation.

Career Growth Path of a Soldier

Sepoy

Initial rank upon joining the Army after training.

Naik

Promoted after a few years of service. Commands a section (small group of soldiers).

Havildar

Second-in-command of a platoon. A position of significant responsibility.

Naib Subedar (JCO)

Promotion to the Junior Commissioned Officer ranks. Commands a platoon.

Subedar & Subedar Major

Highest ranks for a JCO, acting as a crucial link between officers and soldiers.
