Step 1: PFT & PMT

The Physical Fitness Test (PFT) is the first hurdle. It includes a 1.6 km run, pull-ups, a 9-feet ditch jump, and a zig-zag balance test. This is followed by the Physical Measurement Test (PMT) for height, weight, and chest. This is a qualifying stage with marks awarded for the run and pull-ups.

Step 2: Medical Test

Candidates who clear the PFT and PMT undergo a detailed medical examination by Army doctors to ensure they are free from any disease or disability and meet the stringent medical standards required for military service.

Step 3: Written Exam (CEE)

Medically fit candidates appear for the Common Entrance Examination (CEE). It is an objective-type exam testing basic knowledge. The subjects vary by trade. The marks from this exam are crucial for the final merit list.

Final Selection & Training

A final merit list is prepared based on the total marks from the PFT and CEE. Candidates are selected in order of merit. Those selected are dispatched to various regimental training centers for Basic Military Training (BMT).

Step 4: Final Merit List

The final merit list is prepared based on the aggregate marks obtained in the Physical Fitness Test (PFT) and the Common Entrance Examination (CEE). Candidates who rank high enough in the merit list are selected for enlistment.

The Warrior (Soldier GD) ‚öîÔ∏è

The core fighting force. Duties include frontline combat, patrolling, and security operations in arms like Infantry and Artillery.

Example: A GD soldier in an Infantry battalion could be part of a team conducting a reconnaissance patrol along the Line of Control (LoC).

The Technician (Soldier Technical) üõ†Ô∏è

Responsible for the maintenance and repair of complex military equipment, including vehicles, communication systems, and weaponry.

Example: A technician in the EME corps would be tasked with repairing the communication system of a battle tank to ensure it's ready for an exercise.

The Administrator (Soldier Clerk/SKT) ‚úçÔ∏è

Handles all clerical and administrative duties in the unit, such as maintaining records, managing correspondence, and handling stores and inventory.

Example: A clerk is responsible for preparing the monthly salary statements and maintaining the service records of all soldiers in the company office.

The Medic (Soldier NA) ‚öïÔ∏è

A trained paramedic who provides first aid and nursing care to soldiers in military hospitals and field ambulances. The NA (Vet) cares for army animals.

Example: In a high-altitude post, a Nursing Assistant would be responsible for providing emergency medical aid to a soldier suffering from frostbite before evacuation.

The Specialist (Soldier Tradesman) üë∑

Skilled professionals who perform specific jobs like chef, tailor, carpenter, blacksmith, etc. They provide essential support services to keep the unit running smoothly.

Example: A tradesman (Chef) is responsible for preparing hygienic and nutritious meals for hundreds of soldiers in the unit cookhouse every day.

Career Growth Path of a Soldier

Sepoy

Initial rank upon joining the Army after training.

Naik

Promoted after a few years of service. Commands a section (small group of soldiers).

Havildar

Second-in-command of a platoon. A position of significant responsibility.

Naib Subedar (JCO)

Promotion to the Junior Commissioned Officer ranks. Commands a platoon.

Subedar & Subedar Major

Highest ranks for a JCO, acting as a crucial link between officers and soldiers.
