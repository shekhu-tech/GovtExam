1. What is NRA CET (12th Pass)? üí°

The NRA CET for Higher Secondary (12th Pass) level is a unified preliminary exam for various non-gazetted government posts. It replaces the initial screening tests of major recruitment bodies like SSC, RRBs, and IBPS for posts requiring a 10+2 qualification. This single-window exam simplifies the application process, making it easier for millions of students to apply for a wide range of clerical and assistant-level government jobs.

8. Your Preparation Strategy üìà

A systematic and disciplined approach is essential to cover the syllabus and develop the speed required for the CET.

üìö Key Steps:

  1. Understand the Syllabus

    Go through the detailed syllabus for each of the four subjects and identify important topics.

  2. Build Strong Fundamentals

    Use NCERT books from Class 9 to 12 to build a strong foundation, especially for Quantitative Aptitude and General Awareness.

  3. Practice & Revise

    After covering a topic, solve previous year questions and take sectional tests. Regular revision is key to retention.

üîÑ Habits & Practice:

  • Solve Mock Tests: Dedicate the last 1-2 months before the exam exclusively to solving full-length mock tests and analyzing your performance.
  • Current Affairs: Make it a habit to read a newspaper daily and follow a monthly current affairs magazine.
  • Typing Practice: If you are targeting CHSL, practice typing for at least 30 minutes daily.

Stage 1: NRA Common Eligibility Test (CET)

This is the mandatory preliminary screening test. It will be an online objective exam with a common syllabus for all 12th-level posts, covering Reasoning, Quantitative Aptitude, English, and General Awareness. Your CET score will be valid for three years.

Stage 2: Agency-Specific Mains Exam

After qualifying CET, you can apply for the Mains exam for specific posts (like SSC CHSL Tier-II, RRB NTPC CBT-2, IBPS Clerk Mains) conducted by the respective agencies. The pattern and syllabus for this stage will be decided by the agency (SSC/RRB/IBPS).

Stage 3: Skill Test

This is the final stage for many clerical posts. For example, the SSC CHSL exam has a mandatory Typing Test / Data Entry Skill Test (DEST) after Tier-II, which is qualifying in nature. There is usually no interview for these 12th-level posts.
