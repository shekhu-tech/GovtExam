5. Mains Exam Details üìã

The Mains exam is post-specific and tests your in-depth knowledge of the relevant subjects. It often includes both objective and descriptive sections, requiring a mix of knowledge and writing skills.

Exam Structure

The Mains exam for Development Assistant and other posts consists of two papers: one objective and one descriptive. The descriptive paper is a test of your English writing skills, including essay and letter writing.

Key Subjects

Key subjects include **Economic and Social Issues (ESI)** and **Agriculture and Rural Development (ARD)**, which are specific to the NABARD exam. These subjects require a strong understanding of government policies and schemes.

6. Post-wise Details & Duties üåü

  • Providing support to senior officers.

Research Officer

  • Analyzing data and preparing reports.
  • Developing new strategies for rural development.

Project Scientist

  • Working on specific projects related to agriculture.
  • Field-based research and implementation.
  • Collaborating with local farmers and communities.

8. Your Preparation Strategy üìà

Success in the NABARD exam requires a structured and consistent approach. Proper planning and dedicated practice are the keys to cracking the exam.

üìö Key Steps:

  1. Understand the Syllabus

    Thoroughly go through the official syllabus and exam pattern for both Prelims and Mains papers.

  2. Focus on ESI and ARD

    These subjects are unique to NABARD. Dedicate significant time to them and stay updated on government policies and reports.

  3. Practice with Mock Tests

    Regularly take full-length mock tests to get familiar with the exam environment and improve time management.

üîÑ Habits & Practice:

  • Daily Reading: Read newspapers like The Hindu or Indian Express to stay updated on current affairs and improve English.
  • Solve Previous Year Papers: This gives you a clear idea of the type of questions asked and the difficulty level.
  • Focus on Descriptive Writing: Practice writing essays, letters, and reports to score well in the Mains descriptive paper.
  • Revision: Regularly revise what you have studied. This helps in long-term retention of information.

Phase I: Preliminary Exam

This is a common objective-type exam for all posts. It acts as a screening test to shortlist candidates for the Mains. The paper consists of 200 questions across various sections to be completed in 120 minutes. There is a negative marking of 0.25 for each wrong answer.

Phase II: Mains Exam

The Mains exam is specific to the post you have applied for and tests your in-depth knowledge of the relevant subjects. It often includes both objective and descriptive papers. The descriptive paper tests your writing skills in English, especially for the Development Assistant post.

Phase III: Interview

Candidates who qualify in the Mains exam are called for a personal interview. The interview assesses your personality, communication skills, and general suitability for the role and NABARD's mission.
