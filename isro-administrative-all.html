5. Written Exam: Your First Launch üéØ

The written exam is a crucial step to get shortlisted. It tests a candidate's general aptitude, reasoning, and domain-specific knowledge.

1. Exam Pattern

The exam consists of both objective and descriptive questions. The objective part is a screening test for the descriptive part.

  • Negative marking is applicable for objective questions.
  • Syllabus is typically related to the specific post.
  • The exam is usually conducted in multiple choice format followed by a descriptive part for shortlisted candidates.

2. Key Objectives

The main objective is to assess the candidate's general knowledge and understanding of their domain.

  • Evaluate General English and Reasoning abilities.
  • Test knowledge on the specific cadre (e.g., Accounts, Administration).
  • Assess writing and comprehension skills through descriptive tests.

6. Specific Post Requirements üìù

8. Preparation Strategy: Charting Your Course üìà

A focused and disciplined approach is essential for success. Here are some key steps to guide your preparation.

üìö Key Steps:

  1. Understand the Syllabus

    Thoroughly analyze the syllabus for your specific post. Focus on high-weightage topics and identify your strengths and weaknesses.

  2. Practice & Mock Tests

    Regularly solve previous year's question papers and take mock tests. This helps in time management and familiarizes you with the exam pattern.

  3. Stay Updated

    Read newspapers and magazines to stay informed about current affairs, especially in the fields of space science, technology, and governance.

üí° Habits for Success:

  • Improve Typing Speed: Practice typing daily, especially for secretarial posts like UDC and JPA.

  • Maintain a Positive Mindset: Believe in your abilities and stay motivated throughout the preparation journey.
