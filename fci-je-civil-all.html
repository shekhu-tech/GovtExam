Selected candidates for Category III posts undergo a standard probation period to get acclimatized to the work culture and responsibilities at FCI.

8. Your Preparation Strategy: Smart Work üìà

A focused preparation strategy is key. For JEs, it's about balancing general subjects with technical knowledge. For Typists, it's about acing the written test and the skill test.

üìö For JE Aspirants:

  1. Strengthen Engineering Basics

    Your technical paper in Phase-II is the deciding factor. Thoroughly revise your diploma/degree level subjects.

  2. Practice General Subjects

    Don't neglect the Phase-I subjects. Consistent practice is needed for good scores in English, Reasoning, and Quant.

üîÑ For Typist Aspirants:

  • Daily Typing Practice: Dedicate at least one hour daily to improve your Hindi typing speed and accuracy. Use online tools and practice with official layouts.
  • Focus on Phase-II Subjects: Prepare well for English, GK, Reasoning, and Computer Knowledge as these will be part of your written exam.
  • Mock Tests are Key: Take mock tests for both the written exam and the typing test to get used to the time pressure.

Phase I: Common Online Exam

This is a qualifying exam consisting of 100 questions to be solved in 60 minutes. The sections are English, Reasoning, Numerical Aptitude, and General Studies. Marks from Phase-I are not counted for the final merit list, but candidates must clear the cut-off to qualify for Phase-II.

Phase II: Main Online Exam

Phase II is the main selection stage. For JE posts, it consists of a single paper with questions from the relevant engineering discipline. For Typist posts, the paper consists of questions on General English, GA, Reasoning, and Computer Knowledge. Marks from Phase-II are considered for the final merit list.

Skill Test (For Typist)

Candidates who apply for Typist (Hindi) and qualify in the Phase-II written exam will be called for a skill test. This will be a typing test in Hindi to assess their speed and accuracy. The skill test is qualifying in nature. There is no interview for Category III posts.
