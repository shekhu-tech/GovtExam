8. Revision and Mock Tests üîÑ

Revisiting concepts and practicing under timed conditions are critical for success.

  • Less is More: Rely on a few trusted sources and revise them thoroughly rather than using many different books.
  • Solve PYQs: Solving Previous Year Question Papers (PYQs) helps you understand the exam pattern and identify important topics.
  • Mock Tests: Take regular mock tests to improve your speed and time management.

9. Exam Day Rules & Regulations üìã

Following the rules on the exam day ensures a smooth and stress-free experience.

  • Admit Card & ID: It is mandatory to carry your admit card and school ID. Without them, entry is not permitted.
  • Prohibited Items: Mobile phones, smartwatches, and any electronic devices are strictly prohibited inside the exam hall.
  • Time Management: Utilize the 15-minute reading time wisely. Manage your time to attempt all questions.

Theory Papers

The theory component is the main written examination conducted by the board. It is generally 80 marks for most subjects in both Class 10 and 12.

  • Question Types: The paper includes a mix of Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs), short answer questions, long answer questions, and competency-based questions (case studies).
  • Duration: The duration for most papers is 3 hours.

Internal Assessment

Internal assessment is an integral part of the evaluation process, carrying a weightage of 20 marks. It is conducted at the school level.

  • Components: It includes periodic tests (pen-paper based), multiple assessments (quizzes, debates), portfolio submission, and subject enrichment activities (practical/project work).
  • Significance: It evaluates a student's holistic development, including skills, project work, and consistent performance throughout the year.
