  • üéì

  • üéÇ

Medical & Physical Standards üìä

Stringent medical and physical standards are a prerequisite for joining the IAF.

  • Height: Minimum height requirement is 152.5 cm. Certain trades have different requirements.
  • Vision: Must meet specific visual standards, with or without glasses, depending on the trade. Color blindness is a rejection criterion for most trades.
  • Hearing & Dental: Candidates should have normal hearing and a minimum number of healthy teeth.

5. Training: Forging of an Airman ‚úàÔ∏è

Selected candidates undergo a transformative training process, starting with Joint Basic Phase Training (JBPT) and followed by specialized trade training.

1. Joint Basic Phase Training (JBPT)

This initial phase is common for all trainees and is conducted at Basic Training Institute (BTI), Belagavi.

  • Physical Fitness and Drill
  • Weapon Handling and Firing
  • Military Discipline and Ethos
  • General Service and Aviation Knowledge

2. Trade Training

After JBPT, Airmen are sent to different technical and non-technical training institutes for specialization.

  • Hands-on training on aircraft and equipment.
  • Classroom instruction on technical subjects.
  • Training for administrative, logistics, and support roles.

6. Airmen Trade Groups üåü

Airmen are categorized into two main groups based on their roles: Group X (Technical) and Group Y (Non-Technical).

Group X (Technical)

  • Electronics Fitter
  • Electrical Fitter
  • Weapon Fitter
  • Mechanical Fitter

Group Y (Non-Technical)

  • Admin Assistant
  • Accounts Assistant
  • Logistics Assistant
  • IAF Police & Security

Group Y (Special Trades)

  • Musician
  • Medical Assistant
  • Communication Technician

üí° Why is this important?

Your choice of Group X or Y depends on your educational background and determines your career path in the IAF.

8. Preparation Strategy: Aim High üìà

A systematic preparation plan is essential to clear the competitive STAR examination and subsequent tests.

üìö Key Steps:

  1. Master the Syllabus

    Focus on 10+2 level CBSE syllabus for English, Physics, and Maths (Group X) and English & RAGA (Group Y).

  2. Achieve Peak Physical Fitness

    Consistent training for the 1.6 km run and other exercises in the PFT is non-negotiable.

  3. Practice Online Tests

    Familiarize yourself with the computer-based test format by taking numerous mock tests to enhance speed and accuracy.

üîÑ Habits & Practice:

  • Current Affairs: Stay updated on national and international events for the RAGA section.
  • English Proficiency: Improve your grammar, vocabulary, and comprehension skills.
  • Develop Confidence: Practice speaking on various topics to prepare for the group discussion in Adaptability Test-II.

Phase 1: Online Test (STAR)

The first hurdle is a computer-based test. Group X candidates are tested on 10+2 level English, Physics, and Maths. Group Y candidates are tested on English and Reasoning & General Awareness (RAGA).

Phase 2: Verification, PFT & Adaptability Tests

Shortlisted candidates are called to an Airmen Selection Centre for document verification, followed by a Physical Fitness Test (PFT). Those who pass the PFT then take Adaptability Tests I & II to assess their suitability for military life.

Phase 3: Medical Examination

Candidates who clear Phase II undergo a comprehensive medical examination at a designated military hospital to ensure they meet the stringent medical standards of the Indian Air Force.
