1. Work & Projects

Interns work on live projects and assignments.

  • Assigned to a specific department like Market Regulation, Legal Affairs, or IT.

  • To create a pool of trained professionals for the financial sector.

8. How to Prepare Your Application üìà

A successful application hinges on a well-prepared resume, a tailored cover letter or SOP, and strong interview preparation.

üìö Key Application Steps:

  1. Craft a Professional CV

    Highlight your academic credentials, relevant coursework, projects, and any prior internships or publications.

  2. Write a Compelling SOP

    Clearly articulate why you want to intern at SEBI, what skills you bring, and what you hope to learn. For research programs, this will be a detailed research proposal.

  3. Prepare for the Interview

    Deeply revise your core academic subjects. Research SEBI's mandate, recent regulations, and current events in the financial markets.

üîÑ Habits & Practice:

  • Read SEBI's Annual Report: Understand the organization's structure, functions, and priorities.
  • Follow Financial News: Keep up-to-date with market trends and regulatory developments.
  • Refine Your Fundamentals: Be prepared to answer in-depth questions from your core subjects (e.g., Company Law for law students, Financial Derivatives for finance students).
  • Practice Mock Interviews: Articulate your thoughts clearly and confidently.

Stage 1: Application

The process begins with submitting a detailed online application via the SEBI website. This requires uploading an updated CV, academic transcripts, and a compelling Statement of Purpose (SOP) or a detailed research proposal for the fellowship program.

Stage 2: Shortlisting

SEBI's internal committees scrutinize all applications. Candidates are shortlisted for the interview based on their academic excellence, the relevance of their coursework and projects, and the quality and sincerity demonstrated in their SOP or research proposal.

Stage 3: Interview

The final stage is a personal interview with a panel of SEBI officials and domain experts. The interview evaluates the candidate's subject matter knowledge, communication skills, analytical ability, and genuine interest in the securities market. Final selection is based on the performance in this interview.
