5. Training & Job Profile ‚úàÔ∏è

Selected candidates are appointed after successful document verification and medical examination.

1. Job Profile and Duties

MTS staff perform non-clerical, support-level tasks.

  • General cleanliness and upkeep of the office/lab.
  • Carrying of files and other papers within the building.
  • Assisting in routine office/lab work.
  • Other non-clerical work assigned by superiors.

2. Posting and Placement

MTS are liable to be posted anywhere in India in any of DRDO's laboratories or establishments.

  • Induction training is provided after joining.
  • Probation period is typically for two years.

6. MTS is a General Post üåü

Unlike STA-B and Tech-A, the MTS post is not discipline or trade-specific. The recruitment is for a general cadre of staff who can perform a variety of multi-tasking duties as required by the administration in any DRDO lab across the country.

Tier-I: Screening CBT

This is the first stage, a Computer Based Test for screening candidates. It consists of sections on General Intelligence & Reasoning Ability, General Awareness, and Quantitative Aptitude. This test is qualifying, and marks are not counted for the final merit list.

Tier-II: Final Selection CBT

Candidates shortlisted from Tier-I appear for the Tier-II CBT. This test consists of sections on General Science, General Math, and General English. The marks obtained in Tier-II are used to prepare the final merit list for selection.

Final Selection & Verification

The final selection is based purely on the merit of the Tier-II exam. There is no interview. Selected candidates are called for document verification, where their original certificates are checked, followed by a medical examination.
