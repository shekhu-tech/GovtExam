Eligibility Criteria

The first step is to ensure you meet the basic eligibility criteria for age, education, and NCC service.

Example: A 22-year-old graduate with 60% aggregate marks, who is unmarried and holds an NCC 'C' Certificate with 'B' grade, is eligible to apply.

Required Documents

You will need to submit a set of documents during the application and interview stages.

Example: Graduation degree/provisional certificate, NCC 'C' certificate, and a valid photo ID are some of the key documents required.

Shortlisting Criteria

The Army shortlists candidates based on their graduation marks. While there's no fixed cut-off, higher marks improve your chances of getting a call-up for the SSB interview.

Example: In a particular course, candidates with 60% and above in graduation might be shortlisted, depending on the number of applications and vacancies.

Call-up Letter

Once the shortlisting is complete, call-up letters with the SSB dates and venue details are sent to the candidates' registered email addresses.

Example: You will receive a call-up letter inviting you to appear for the SSB at a center like Allahabad, Bhopal, or Bangalore on a specific date.

Day 1: Screening Test

This is the first day of the SSB. It includes two tests: the Officer Intelligence Rating (OIR) test and the Picture Perception and Discussion Test (PPDT).

Example: In the PPDT, you are shown a blurry picture for 30 seconds and then have to write a story about it. Afterwards, you discuss your story with a group of other candidates.

Day 2: Psychological Test

This is a series of written tests designed to assess your mental state and emotional stability. It includes TAT, WAT, SRT, and SD.

Example: In the Word Association Test (WAT), you are shown a word for 15 seconds and have to write a sentence that comes to your mind first. This tests your subconscious thought process.

Day 3-4: GTO Test

The Group Testing Officer (GTO) test is a series of outdoor tasks that assess your social skills, leadership, and teamwork. It includes GD, GPE, PGT, and HGT.

Example: In the Progressive Group Task (PGT), your group has to cross a series of obstacles using planks and ropes, showcasing your ability to work with others to solve a problem.

Day 5: Conference

This is the final day where you have a brief interaction with all the assessors. They review your performance and make a final recommendation.

Example: You will sit in front of the board of officers and they may ask a few general questions like "How was your stay?" or "What did you learn from the process?"

Physical Standards

You must meet the minimum physical standards for height and weight. There are also specific requirements for physical fitness.

Example: A male candidate for the Indian Army must be at least 157.5 cm tall and have a proportionate weight. Physical tests like running and push-ups are also part of the assessment.

Medical Standards

A thorough medical check-up is conducted to ensure you are free from any chronic diseases or physical deformities. This includes tests for vision, hearing, and dental health.

Example: A candidate with flat feet or a history of major surgery may be deemed medically unfit. Clear vision within acceptable limits is a common requirement.

Final Selection and Training

After successfully clearing all stages, a final merit list is created based on SSB marks. Selected candidates join the training academy.

  • Training Academy: Officers Training Academy (OTA) in Chennai.
  • Training Period: Approximately 49 weeks.
  • Focus: Military training, leadership development, and physical fitness.

Example: A candidate who makes the merit list must report to the OTA in Chennai for their training. This training includes rigorous physical exercise, tactical training, and academic studies to prepare them for commissioning as an Officer.

No Written Exam ‚úÖ

The biggest advantage is that candidates do not have to appear for the Combined Defence Services (CDS) written examination. Your dedication in NCC is valued directly.

Example: While thousands of aspirants prepare for the tough CDS exam, as an NCC 'C' Certificate holder, you can focus entirely on preparing for the SSB interview.

Direct SSB Interview üéØ

Shortlisted candidates get a direct call for the 5-day Service Selection Board (SSB) interview, which is the main hurdle in the selection process.

Example: After applying online, if your graduation marks meet the criteria, you will receive a call-up letter to attend the SSB at one of the selection centers.

Reserved Vacancies

A specific number of vacancies in the training academies are reserved for NCC cadets, which reduces the overall competition compared to other entries.

Example: Each course has a set number of seats (e.g., 50 for men, 5 for women) exclusively for NCC cadets, meaning you are competing only against other NCC candidates.

Age Relaxation (Wards of Battle Casualties) üóìÔ∏è

For the wards of battle casualties, there is a provision for relaxation in the upper age limit, providing them with a better opportunity to join the forces.

Example: If the normal age limit is 25, a ward of a battle casualty might be eligible to apply up to a certain extended age, as per the notification.
