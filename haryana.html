4. HSSC CET Exam Pattern üìö

The Haryana CET for Group C & D posts has a specific structure with significant weightage given to Haryana-related subjects.

General Aptitude (75%)

Includes General Awareness, Reasoning, Maths, Science, English, Hindi, and Computer Knowledge.

Haryana GK (25%)

A dedicated section covering the History, Geography, Civics, Environment, and Culture of Haryana.

Socio-Economic Criteria

Additional weightage (e.g., 5 marks) may be given based on specific socio-economic criteria as defined by the government.

Stage 1: Written Test (CET)

The first stage is the Common Eligibility Test (CET) conducted by HSSC. It is an objective MCQ test covering General Awareness, Reasoning, Maths, Science, Hindi, English, Computer, and a significant 25% section on Haryana GK. A good score in CET is mandatory to be eligible for the next stage.

Stage 2: Mains / Skill Test

Based on the CET score, HSSC calls candidates for a second-stage exam for specific posts like Clerk. For roles like Data Entry Operator, this stage is a mandatory Skill Test (e.g., HARTRON SETC), where candidates must meet the required typing speed and accuracy. This stage is often qualifying in nature.

Stage 3: Document Verification

Candidates who clear all the preceding stages are called for Document Verification. All original certificates related to education, age, Haryana Domicile, and category are verified. The final merit list is typically prepared based on the marks from the written exam and any applicable socio-economic criteria.
