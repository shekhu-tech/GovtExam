1. What is NRA CET (Graduate Level)? üí°

The NRA CET for Graduate Level is a single, unified preliminary examination for various non-gazetted Group 'B' and 'C' posts that require a Bachelor's degree. It's set to replace the initial screening tests (Tier-I/Prelims) of major exams like the SSC CGL, RRB NTPC (Graduate Posts), and IBPS PO/Clerk. This reform simplifies the recruitment process, allowing graduates to take one exam to become eligible for a multitude of prestigious government jobs.

8. Your Preparation Strategy üìà

An integrated preparation strategy that covers both the CET and the potential Mains exams is the most effective approach.

üìö Key Steps:

  1. Build a Strong Foundation

    Use NCERTs and standard reference books to build a solid base in all CET subjects. For CGL Mains, advanced maths is also crucial.

  2. Focus on Mains-level Subjects

    Prepare for subjects like Descriptive English, Banking Awareness (for IBPS), and advanced syllabus topics for SSC/RRB right from the beginning.

  3. Practice and Mock Tests

    Regularly take mock tests for both CET and the specific Mains exam you are targeting to develop a robust exam-taking strategy.

üîÑ Habits & Practice:

  • Read Editorials: Daily reading of newspaper editorials (e.g., The Hindu) is essential for descriptive English and interview preparation.
  • Current Affairs: Follow daily and monthly current affairs compilations, with a special focus on economic and financial news.
  • Solve Previous Year Papers: Solve past papers of CGL, NTPC, and IBPS PO to understand the level and pattern of the Mains exams.

Stage 1: NRA Common Eligibility Test (CET)

This will be the first-level screening test for all graduate-level posts. It will be a single online exam testing Reasoning, Quantitative Aptitude, English, and General Awareness at a graduate level of difficulty. The score will be valid for 3 years.

Stage 2: Agency-Specific Mains Exam

Based on your CET score, you can apply for the Mains/Tier-II exams of specific posts like SSC CGL, RRB NTPC, or IBPS PO. This stage will have a specialized and more in-depth syllabus set by the respective recruiting agency (SSC, RRB, or IBPS).

Stage 3: Interview / Final Stage

This is the final stage for most officer-level posts. It may include a Descriptive Paper, Group Discussion, and/or a Personal Interview. This stage assesses your personality, communication skills, and suitability for an officer's role. The final merit is prepared by the recruiting agency.
