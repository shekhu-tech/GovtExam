5. Training at BARC Training Schools ‚öõÔ∏è

Selected candidates, known as Trainee Scientific Officers (TSOs), undergo a one-year, high-intensity orientation course at one of the five BARC Training Schools.

1. The Curriculum

The training is a blend of advanced coursework, hands-on experiments, and project work.

  • Advanced courses in your discipline.
  • Core courses on nuclear science and engineering.
  • Practical training in labs and facilities.
  • Project work on real-world DAE problems.

2. Key Objectives

The goal is to provide TSOs with the necessary knowledge and skills for a successful R&D career in the DAE.

  • Bridge the gap between academic knowledge and DAE's requirements.
  • Instill a strong research and development culture.
  • Provide a broad, multi-disciplinary perspective.
  • Foster a spirit of scientific inquiry and innovation.

Screening Details

Candidates are shortlisted for interviews based on their performance in the BARC Online Examination OR their GATE score. The choice between these two screening options must be made at the time of application.

Selection Interview

Shortlisted candidates attend a selection interview at the BARC Training School, Mumbai. It is a purely technical interview where the panel probes the fundamental concepts of your chosen subjects.

Final Selection

Final selection is based solely on performance in the selection interview. There is no weightage for the online exam or GATE score in the final list. Selected candidates are designated as Scientific Officer 'C'.
