5. Training & Probation üèõÔ∏è

All new trainees at SAIL undergo a mandatory training and probation period to learn the intricacies of steel manufacturing and the company's work culture.

1. Training Period

The duration and nature of training vary by post.

  • Management Trainees undergo one year of training.
  • OCTT and ACTT candidates undergo a two-year training period.
  • Training includes both classroom sessions and extensive on-the-job training at various plant locations.

2. Probation & Placement

After training, successful candidates are absorbed into the regular grades.

  • Upon successful completion of training, candidates are placed on probation for one year.
  • They are absorbed into regular posts and pay scales (e.g., MTs at E-1 grade, OCTTs at S-3 grade).
  • Final posting is at one of SAIL's integrated steel plants or other units.

8. Your Preparation Strategy üìà

Success hinges on building a strong foundation in your specific technical domain while also preparing for the general aptitude sections.

üìö For Technical Posts (MT/OCTT/ACTT):

  1. Master Your Domain

    The technical paper holds the highest weightage. Thoroughly revise your entire B.Tech/Diploma/ITI syllabus, focusing on core subjects relevant to the steel industry (e.g., Metallurgy, Mechanical).

  2. Practice Aptitude Questions

    Regularly solve questions on reasoning, quantitative aptitude, and English to score well in the general paper.

üîÑ For All Posts:

  • Solve Previous SAIL Papers: This is the best way to understand the exam's difficulty level and the types of questions asked.
  • Prepare for Skill/Trade Test: For OCTT/ACTT, practical knowledge is key. Be prepared for a hands-on test of your trade skills.
  • Prepare for GD/Interview: Research SAIL, its various steel plants, its products, and recent news about the company and the steel sector.

9. Recruitment Cycle & Process üóìÔ∏è

SAIL announces recruitment drives based on the manpower requirements of its different plants and units across the country.

Notification Release

Timeline: Vacancies are advertised on the SAIL website (sail.co.in) under the "Careers" section.

Written Examination

Timeline: A Computer Based Test (CBT) is conducted a month or two after the application period closes.

Skill Test / GD & Interview

Timeline: Shortlisted candidates are called for the second stage. The weightage for Written Test, GD, and Interview is typically 75:10:15 for MTs.

Final Result & Medicals

Timeline: A final merit list is published, followed by a rigorous pre-employment medical examination at a company hospital.

Stage 1: Written Test

This is an online Computer Based Test (CBT). It typically consists of two parts: a general aptitude paper (testing reasoning, quantitative ability, English, and GK) and a domain knowledge paper with questions from the relevant technical/professional discipline.

Stage 2: GD / Skill Test

For Management Trainees, shortlisted candidates are called for a Group Discussion (GD). For technical posts like Operator-cum-Technician and Attendant-cum-Technician, a Skill Test or Trade Test is conducted to assess the practical skills of the candidates.

Stage 3: Interview

The final stage for executive and medical posts is a personal interview. A panel assesses the candidate's technical knowledge, managerial competencies, and overall personality fit for the organization. For non-executive posts, the selection is often based on the written and skill test marks only.
