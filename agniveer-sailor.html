3. Who Can Apply? ‚úÖ

Eligibility criteria vary by entry type (Agniveer SSR, MR, etc.). The fundamental requirements are:

  • üéì
    Education: Minimum 10th or 12th pass, depending on the entry. Specific subjects (e.g., PCM for SSR) may be required.
  • üéÇ
    Age: Typically between **17.5 and 23 years** for Agniveer entries. Refer to the official notification for exact dates.
  • üáÆüá≥
    Nationality: Must be an Indian citizen.

Physical & Medical Standards üìä

Candidates must be medically and physically fit as per the stringent standards of the Indian Navy.

  • Height & Weight: Specific minimum height requirements (e.g., 157 cm for male candidates) with weight proportionate to height.
  • Vision: Must meet the specified vision standards. For some entries like SSR, specific unaided and aided vision standards apply.
  • Physical Fitness Test (PFT): Includes running (e.g., 1.6 km in 6 min 30 sec), squats, and push-ups.

Number of Attempts:

There is no limit on the number of attempts as long as you meet the age and educational criteria for the specific notification.

5. Agniveer (MR) ‚öì

Agniveer (MR) provides an entry for candidates who have passed 10th grade. They serve as sailors in roles such as Chef, Steward, and Hygienist.

1. Eligibility & Roles

Training is a comprehensive blend of academic, outdoor, and naval-specific subjects.

  • Education: 10th pass.
  • Age: 17.5 to 23 years.
  • Roles: Chef, Steward, Hygienist.

2. Key Objectives

The goal is to develop a capable sailor who can contribute to the Navy's efficiency and mission readiness.

  • Serve the naval fleet and shore establishments.
  • Learn essential seamanship and discipline.
  • Contribute to the well-being of the ship's crew.
  • Opportunity for permanent enrolment based on performance.

6. Other Sailor Entry Schemes üåü

Beyond Agniveer, the Indian Navy also recruits through other entries like Musician and Artificer Apprentice, each with its own specific eligibility and selection process.

Navy Musician Entry

  • Education: 10th pass.
  • Musical ability: Proficiency in musical instruments.
  • Selection: Written exam, Physical Fitness Test, and Music Aptitude Test.

Artificer Apprentice

  • Education: 10+2 with PCM.
  • Age: 17 to 20 years.
  • Selection: Written exam, PFT, Medical.

8. Your Preparation Strategy üìà

A structured and disciplined approach is crucial for clearing the Navy's selection tests. Focus on both written exam preparation and physical fitness.

üìö Written Exam:

  1. Study the Syllabus

    Familiarize yourself with the syllabus for your chosen entry (e.g., SSR or MR). Focus on General Science, Maths, GK, and English.

  2. Practice Previous Papers

    Solve previous years' question papers and mock tests to get a feel for the exam pattern and time management.

  3. Stay Updated

    Read newspapers and stay updated on national and international current affairs to ace the GK section.

üèÉ PFT & Medical:

  • Run Daily: Practice running the required distance (1.6 km) in the stipulated time. Build your stamina gradually.
  • Strength Training: Regularly do squats (Uthak Baithak) and push-ups to build physical strength.
  • Medical Check-up: Get a pre-recruitment medical check-up to identify and correct any issues in advance.
  • Maintain Health: Follow a healthy diet and lifestyle to ensure you are in peak physical condition.

9. Training at INS Chilka üóìÔ∏è

Selected candidates for sailor/agniveer entries undergo basic training at INS Chilka, Odisha. The training is a transformative experience designed to turn a civilian into a professional sailor.

Basic Training

Timeline: Around 10-14 weeks, focusing on physical fitness, parade, discipline, and basic naval subjects.

Trade Training

Timeline: Post-basic training, you are assigned a specific trade and undergo specialized training for that role.

On-the-Job Training

Timeline: After trade training, you are posted to a ship or shore establishment to apply your skills in a real-world environment.

Career & Enrolment

Timeline: After four years as an Agniveer, up to 25% are selected for permanent enrolment based on performance and merit.
