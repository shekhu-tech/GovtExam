5. Training & Probation üèõÔ∏è

New recruits undergo a probation period where they receive on-the-job training to understand their duties and the hospital environment.

1. Probation Period

New joiners are confirmed in service after a probationary period.

  • A probation period of up to two years is common.
  • Performance is assessed based on diligence, conduct, and learning ability.
  • Confirmation is subject to satisfactory performance.

2. On-the-Job Training

Practical, hands-on training is provided from day one.

  • Orientation on hospital rules, hygiene, and safety protocols.
  • Direct training under the supervision of senior staff.
  • Learning the specific duties related to their post (e.g., patient care, cooking, etc.).

6. Key Responsibilities of Support Staff üåü

These roles are hands-on and crucial for the daily functioning of a medical facility.

Dresser / Nursing Orderly

  • Cleaning and dressing wounds as directed.
  • Assisting nurses in patient care.
  • Sterilizing and maintaining medical equipment.

Cook / Kitchen Staff

  • Preparing wholesome meals for patients as per diet charts.
  • Maintaining cleanliness and hygiene in the kitchen.
  • Managing kitchen supplies and inventory.

Ward Boy / Attendant

  • Assisting patients with mobility (e.g., on wheelchairs, stretchers).
  • Maintaining cleanliness of the ward and its facilities.
  • Running errands for the nursing staff.

Written Examination

This is the first and most crucial stage. It is an objective multiple-choice question (MCQ) based test covering subjects like General Intelligence & Reasoning, General Awareness, Numerical Aptitude, and English Comprehension. The level of the exam is typically based on the Matriculation (10th) standard.

Skill / Trade Test

For certain posts like Cook, a skill test may be conducted to assess practical knowledge and proficiency. This test is qualifying in nature, meaning candidates must pass it, but the marks are usually not added to the final merit list. The merit list is prepared based on the marks from the written exam.

Document Verification

Candidates who qualify in the written exam (and skill test, if applicable) are called for document verification. They must produce all original documents to prove their eligibility, including educational certificates, age proof, caste certificates, etc. There is generally no interview for these posts.
