12. Career Growth Path: A Detailed Roadmap üöÄ

The career of an Education Havildar offers a clear progression path to becoming a Junior Commissioned Officer (JCO) based on service and merit.

Step 1: Screening (PFT & PMT)

The first step is the Physical Fitness Test (PFT), which includes a 1.6 km run, pull-ups, zig-zag balance, and a 9-feet ditch jump. This is followed by the Physical Measurement Test (PMT) where your height, weight, and chest are measured. It is a qualifying stage.

Step 2: Written Examination

Qualified candidates appear for the Common Entrance Examination (CEE). It consists of two parts: Part 1 (GK & English) is compulsory. Part 2 is stream-specific (Science/Arts) and tests your subject knowledge. Marks from both parts are used for the merit list.

Step 3: Teaching Aptitude Test / Skill Test

Candidates who pass the written exam undergo a skill test. This test assesses your suitability as a teacher. You may be asked to deliver a short lecture on a topic from your subject to test your communication skills, confidence, and teaching ability. This is also a qualifying stage.

Final Selection & Training

A final merit list is prepared based on the marks in the written exam for candidates who clear all stages. Selected candidates are sent for Basic Military Training (BMT) followed by technical training at the AEC Training College & Centre, Pachmarhi.

Step 4: Final Merit List

The final merit list is prepared based on the aggregate marks obtained in Part 1 and Part 2 of the written examination. Candidates must qualify the physical and skill tests to be considered for the merit list.

Academic Instructor üë®‚Äçüè´

Your main role is to conduct classes for soldiers to help them pass various Army promotion exams (e.g., Army Certificate of Education). You teach subjects like English, Maths, Science, and Social Studies.

Example: You would be running a 3-month course in your unit to prepare soldiers for their 'Map Reading Standard One' exam, a crucial test for their promotion to the NCO ranks.

Map Reading Instructor üó∫Ô∏è

Map reading is a critical military skill. Education Havildars are often the primary instructors for teaching soldiers how to read topographical maps, use a compass, and understand grid references.

Example: Before a major field exercise, you would conduct refresher classes on night navigation and setting up a map to the ground for the entire company.

Unit Education Duties üè¢

You are responsible for managing the unit's Information Room, maintaining records of soldiers' educational qualifications, and encouraging them to enroll in higher education courses.

Example: You would motivate soldiers to enroll in distance learning programs from IGNOU or other universities and guide them through the application process.

Librarian Duties üìö

In many units, the Education Havildar is also in charge of the unit library, responsible for the issue and receipt of books, and maintaining the library's collection.

Example: You would be responsible for procuring new books for the library based on the soldiers' interests and ensuring the reading room is a welcoming space for learning.

Career Growth Path of an Education Havildar

Havildar

Initial rank upon completion of training.

Naib Subedar

Promoted based on seniority and clearing promotion cadres. This is the first rank as a Junior Commissioned Officer (JCO).

Subedar

Further promotion based on service and merit. You take on more administrative and supervisory roles.

Subedar Major

The highest rank for a JCO, achieved after long and distinguished service. You could be posted as the senior JCO of the AEC Centre.
