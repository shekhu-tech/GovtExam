The Stipendiary Trainee Scheme is a recruitment program by BARC to select and train candidates for technical roles. 3. Eligibility Criteria ‚úÖ

To be eligible for the Stipendiary Trainee scheme, you must meet these fundamental criteria:

  • üë®‚Äçüéì
    Education: For **Category-I**, a **Diploma** in a relevant engineering discipline with a minimum of 60% marks. For **Category-II**, a **12th pass with a minimum of 60% marks in Science subjects (Physics, Chemistry, Maths)** or an **ITI certificate** in a relevant trade.
  • üéÇ
    Age: Typically, age limits are **18-24 years** for Category-I and **18-22 years** for Category-II. Age relaxations apply for OBC, SC/ST, and PWD categories.
  • üáÆüá≥
    Nationality: Must be an Indian citizen.

Medical & Physical Standards üìä

Candidates must be medically and physically fit as per the stringent standards of the DAE. A medical examination will be conducted before final selection.

  • Physical Standards: Specific height, weight, and chest measurements.
  • Vision: Must meet the specified vision standards.
  • Overall Health: Free from any disease or disability that could hinder performance.

Number of Attempts:

There is no official limit on the number of attempts for the Stipendiary Trainee posts, as long as you meet the age and educational criteria for that year's notification. You can appear as many times as you are eligible.

5. Training Program at BARC üéì

Selected candidates undergo an intensive two-year training program at various DAE training schools and facilities. The program is designed to transform them into highly skilled technicians and supervisors.

1. The Curriculum

The training is a mix of theoretical sessions and hands-on practical work to build a strong foundation.

  • Classroom sessions on relevant subjects.
  • In-plant training and on-the-job experience.
  • Safety procedures and protocols.
  • Project work and practical examinations.

2. Key Objectives

The goal is to produce highly competent professionals who can contribute to the safe and efficient operation of DAE facilities.

  • Develop practical skills in their respective trades.
  • Inculcate a culture of safety and precision.
  • Prepare trainees for diverse roles in DAE units.
  • Provide an opportunity for career growth.

8. Your Preparation Strategy: Smart Work üìù

Success in the BARC Stipendiary Trainee exams is about targeted practice and reinforcing your fundamentals. A structured approach is key to achieving your goal.

üìö Key Steps:

  1. Master Aptitude

    Focus on general mathematics, science, and reasoning questions. Practice as many mock tests as possible to improve your speed and accuracy.

  2. Strengthen Your Basics

    For the advanced test, revise your Diploma or ITI course material. Focus on core concepts and their applications.

  3. Practice Hands-On Skills

    For the skill test, practice practical tasks related to your trade. Be confident in demonstrating your proficiency in a real-world scenario.

üîÑ Habits & Practice:

  • Solve Previous Papers: Familiarize yourself with the exam pattern and question types by solving previous years' papers.
  • Time Management: Practice solving questions within a strict time limit to prepare for the pressure of the exam.
  • Stay Calm: Maintain your composure during the skill test/interview. A calm and confident demeanor is as important as technical knowledge.

Preliminary Test (Stage 1)

This is a screening test with multiple-choice questions on Mathematics, Science, and General Awareness. It is designed to filter a large number of applicants.

Advanced Test (Stage 2)

The advanced test is a multiple-choice exam on technical subjects specific to your trade or discipline. It is a crucial step for shortlisting candidates for the final stage.

Skill Test / Interview (Stage 3)

This stage is a practical skill test or a viva-voce interview. Candidates are assessed on their hands-on proficiency, theoretical knowledge, and confidence.
