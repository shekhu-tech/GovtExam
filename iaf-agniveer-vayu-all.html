A Step Towards a Future-Ready Air Force

1. Who are Agniveer Vayu? 💡

Agniveer Vayu are personnel enlisted in the Indian Air Force under the Agnipath scheme. This scheme provides an opportunity for India's youth to serve in the armed forces for a period of four years. It's a transformative initiative to create a youthful, tech-savvy, and agile fighting force.

3. Eligibility Criteria ✅

To join as an Agniveer Vayu, candidates must meet the following criteria:

  • 🎓
    Education: Passed **10+2 / Intermediate** with specific subjects. For Science stream, Maths, Physics & English is mandatory. For other streams, any subject approved by Education Boards.
  • 🎂
    Age: Typically between **17.5 and 21 years** at the time of enrolment. The exact age bracket is specified in the official notification.
  • 🇮🇳
    Marital Status: Only unmarried male and female candidates are eligible.

Medical & Physical Standards 📊

Candidates must meet the medical and physical standards prescribed for the IAF.

  • Height: Minimum height requirement is typically 152.5 cm for both males and females.
  • Vision: Must meet the specified visual standards, which vary based on the trade.
  • Overall Health: Must be in good physical and mental health, free from any disease or disability.

5. Training: From Civilian to Air Warrior ✈️

Selected candidates undergo rigorous basic and trade-specific training to be honed into skilled and disciplined Agniveer Vayu.

1. Basic Training

Joint Basic Phase Training is conducted at a designated institute like the Basic Training Institute (BTI), Belagavi.

  • Intense Physical Fitness Regimen
  • Drill and Weapon Training
  • Military Etiquette and Discipline
  • General Service Knowledge

2. Trade Training

After basic training, Agniveers proceed to specialized training centers for their allotted trades.

  • Technical training for trades like aviation, electronics.
  • Non-technical training for admin, logistics, accounts.
  • Focus on developing professional skills for operational roles.

6. Agniveer Vayu Trades 🌟

8. Your Preparation Strategy: Mission Ready 📈

A disciplined and well-rounded preparation strategy covering both the online test and physical fitness is crucial for selection.

📚 Key Steps:

  1. Understand the Syllabus

    Thoroughly go through the syllabus for your stream (Science or Other than Science). Focus on 10+2 level concepts for Physics, Maths, and RAGA.

  2. Build Physical Stamina

    Start your physical training well in advance. Focus on running, push-ups, and sit-ups to meet the PFT standards comfortably.

  3. Practice Mock Tests

    Regularly solve mock tests and previous years' question papers to improve your speed, accuracy, and time management for the online exam.

🔄 Habits & Practice:

  • Stay Informed: Keep yourself updated with current affairs as they form a part of the RAGA section.
  • Improve English: Read regularly to improve your comprehension and vocabulary, which is essential for the English paper.
  • Group Discussion Practice: If possible, practice group discussions with friends on current topics to build confidence for Adaptability Test-II.

9. Recruitment Cycle & Process 🗓️

The Agniveer Vayu recruitment is conducted through online applications followed by selection tests, typically held twice a year. Always check the official website agnipathvayu.cdac.in for the latest notifications.

Notification & Registration

Timeline: A window of a few weeks is provided for online registration.

Phase I: Online Test

Timeline: Conducted a few weeks after the registration closes. Results declare shortlisted candidates for Phase II.

Phase II & III

Timeline: Conducted for shortlisted candidates at designated Airmen Selection Centres (ASCs). This includes PFT, Adaptability Tests, and Medicals.

Final Enrolment List

Timeline: Published after completion of all phases. Selected candidates receive call letters for enrolment and training.

10. Common Mistakes to Avoid 🚫

Many candidates get disqualified due to simple, avoidable mistakes. Being aware of these can make a huge difference.

❌
Ignoring Physical Fitness:

Clearing the online test is useless if you fail the PFT. Start training early.

❌
Poor Time Management:

Not being able to complete the online paper within the given time due to lack of practice.

❌
Lack of Confidence in GD:

Being nervous or not contributing logically in the Group Discussion (Adaptability Test-II).

❌
Ignoring Negative Marking:

Attempting questions through guesswork in the online test can drastically lower your score.

Phase 1: Online Test

This is a computer-based test with objective-type questions. The syllabus covers either Science Subjects (Physics, Maths, English) or Other than Science Subjects (English, Reasoning & General Awareness - RAGA), based on the candidate's 10+2 stream.

Phase 2: Verification, PFT & Adaptability Tests

Candidates who clear the online test are called to an Airmen Selection Centre. It involves document verification, a Physical Fitness Test (PFT), and Adaptability Tests (AT-I and AT-II) to assess suitability for the military environment.

Phase 3: Medical Examination

Candidates who clear Phase II undergo a detailed medical examination at a designated Medical Boarding Centre to ensure they meet the IAF's medical standards.
