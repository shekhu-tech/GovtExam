5. Training & Probation üèõÔ∏è

Selected candidates undergo a structured training program and a probation period to acclimatize them to the work culture and responsibilities at LIC.

1. For AAO (Specialist)

The journey for an Assistant Administrative Officer begins with foundational training.

  • Induction training at a Zonal/Divisional Training Centre.
  • A probation period of one year from the date of joining.
  • Probation may be extended up to two years.
  • Guaranteed bond period of a minimum of four years.

2. For Actuarial Apprentices

Actuarial Apprentices are groomed to become future actuaries of the corporation.

  • Serve as an apprentice for one year, which may be extended.
  • Must pass specified actuarial papers during the apprenticeship.
  • Appointment as AAO (Actuarial) upon successful completion.
  • Stipend paid during the apprenticeship period.

8. Your Preparation Strategy: Smart Work üìà

Success in the LIC AAO exam requires a balanced approach, focusing on speed and accuracy for prelims, and in-depth knowledge for mains. A structured plan is essential.

üìö Key Steps:

  1. Master the Basics

  2. Deep Dive into Professional Subject

    Thoroughly cover the syllabus for your specific specialization. This section has the highest weightage in the Main examination.

  3. Stay Updated

    Regularly follow current affairs, especially news related to the economy, banking, and insurance sectors. This is vital for the GA section and the interview.

üîÑ Habits & Practice:

  • Revise Consistently: Make short notes for formulas, concepts, and current affairs for quick and effective revision.

Phase 1: Preliminary Exam

This is a qualifying online exam consisting of three sections: Reasoning, Quantitative Aptitude, and English Language. Marks in the English section are not counted for ranking. Candidates need to clear the sectional and overall cut-offs to qualify for the Mains exam.

Phase 2: Main Exam

The Main exam includes objective tests on Reasoning, General Knowledge, Professional Knowledge, and Insurance & Financial Market Awareness, along with a descriptive test (Essay and Letter Writing). The final merit list is prepared based on the marks obtained in the Main exam.

Phase 3: Interview & Medicals

Candidates who clear the Main exam are called for a personal interview. The interview assesses their subject knowledge, personality, and suitability for the role. Final selection is subject to clearing a pre-recruitment medical examination.
