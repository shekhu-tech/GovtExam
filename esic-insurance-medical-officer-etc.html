5. Training & Probation üèõÔ∏è

Selected candidates in the medical cadre are appointed on a probationary basis to ensure they meet the standards of patient care and administration at ESIC.

1. Probation Period

A standard probation period applies to all new recruits.

  • Typically a two-year probation period for permanent posts.
  • Performance is evaluated based on clinical duties and administrative responsibilities.
  • Confirmation is based on satisfactory performance.

2. Induction & Orientation

New joiners are oriented to the ESI scheme and its functioning.

  • Orientation on the provisions of the ESI Act.
  • Training on hospital administration and medical procedures specific to ESIC.
  • Continuous Medical Education (CME) opportunities are provided.

6. Key Responsibilities of Medical Cadre üåü

The roles are diverse, ranging from direct patient care to technical support, all aimed at serving the insured population.

IMO / Specialist

  • Providing diagnosis and treatment in OPD/IPD.
  • Performing medical procedures and surgeries.
  • Managing patient care and medical records.

Staff Nurse

  • Assisting doctors and administering medications.
  • Providing bedside nursing care to patients.
  • Maintaining ward hygiene and patient records.

Paramedical Staff

  • Dispensing medicines (Pharmacist).
  • Conducting lab tests (Lab Technician).
  • Operating X-ray/imaging equipment (Radiographer).

8. Your Preparation Strategy: Smart Work üìà

Preparation must be highly focused on the technical/medical syllabus, as this constitutes the major portion of the written test for all posts.

üìö Key Steps:

  1. Master Your Professional Syllabus

    Revise your MBBS/PG or Diploma/Degree subjects thoroughly. This is the highest-scoring section.

  2. Prepare the General Section

    Allocate some time to practice General Aptitude, Reasoning, and Awareness, as these can be rank-deciding.

  3. Solve Previous Papers

    Practice with previous years' question papers of ESIC and other similar medical recruitment exams.

üîÑ Habits & Practice:

  • Stay Updated: Keep abreast of the latest developments in medicine and healthcare for the interview.
  • Focus on Clinical Scenarios: For doctors, questions are often based on clinical case scenarios. Practice these well.
  • Time Management: Practice solving MCQs within a time limit to manage the exam pressure effectively.
  • Group Study: Discussing complex medical topics with peers can help in better understanding and retention.

Written Examination

This is an objective type multiple-choice test. It consists of two parts: a section on general aptitude & awareness, and a major section on the candidate's specific technical/professional discipline (e.g., MBBS syllabus for IMO, Nursing syllabus for Staff Nurse).

Interview (For Officer Posts)

Candidates who clear the written test for Insurance Medical Officer (IMO) and Specialist Grade-II posts are called for a personal interview. The interview assesses their professional knowledge, personality, and suitability for the role. The final merit list is based on the combined marks of the written test and interview.

Document Verification

All candidates who qualify in the final stage (written test for paramedical staff, interview for officers) are required to produce their original documents for verification. This includes educational certificates, registration with medical/nursing councils, experience certificates, and caste certificates.
