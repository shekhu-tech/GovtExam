Eligibility for these AP Government jobs is based on specific educational and age criteria set by the state.

  • üéÇ
    Age Limit: Typically between **18 and 42 years**. Age relaxation for SC/ST/BC/EWS/PWD/Ex-Servicemen is applicable as per Andhra Pradesh Government rules.
  • üéì

Number of Attempts üìä

4. APPSC/DSC Exam Pattern üìö

The written test for Group III/IV posts usually includes General Studies and specific papers on secretarial abilities.

Paper 1: General Studies & Mental Ability

Covers current affairs, general science, Indian history (with emphasis on AP), geography, and reasoning.

Paper 2: General English & General Telugu

Tests language proficiency and comprehension skills up to the SSC (10th) standard.

5. Training & Probation üèõÔ∏è

New recruits are confirmed into government service after successfully completing a mandatory probation period.

1. Probation Period

A standard two-year probation period applies.

  • During probation, the employee's performance and conduct are observed.
  • Successful completion leads to confirmation as a permanent employee.

2. On-the-Job Training

Focuses on departmental procedures and rules.

  • Induction on Andhra Pradesh state service rules.
  • Practical training on administrative tasks, file management, and department-specific protocols.

6. Key Responsibilities üåü

These roles are diverse and form the core of public service delivery at the district level.

Admin & Revenue

  • **Clerk/DEO:** Administrative duties in Collectorates.
  • **Patwari/VRO:** Village-level land record management.
  • **Court Staff:** Supporting the judicial administration.

Public Welfare

  • **ANM/GNM:** Community health services in PHCs.
  • **Anganwadi Staff:** Implementing ICDS for child welfare.
  • **Guest Teacher:** Supporting the education system.

Local Governance

  • **Panchayat Sahayak:** Assisting Gram Panchayats.
  • **Municipal Staff:** Providing civic services in towns and cities.
  • **DRDA Staff:** Implementing rural development projects.

9. Recruitment Cycle & Process üóìÔ∏è

Recruitment for these posts is conducted by APPSC or District Selection Committees (DSCs) based on the vacancies reported by various departments.

Notification Release

Timeline: Vacancies are advertised on the official APPSC website (psc.ap.gov.in) and in major newspapers.

Written Examination

Timeline: A written test (often online) is conducted a few months after the application process closes.

Skill Test & Document Verification

Timeline: Shortlisted candidates are called for computer proficiency/typing tests (if applicable) and certificate verification.

Final Merit List & Appointment

Timeline: The final selection list is prepared based on written test marks, and appointment orders are issued by the respective district authorities.
