Step 1: Screening (PFT & PMT)

The first stage involves checking documents, followed by the Physical Fitness Test (PFT) and Physical Measurement Test (PMT). The PFT includes a 1.6 km run, pull-ups, etc. You must qualify this stage to proceed.

Step 2: Written Examination

Candidates who clear screening appear for a written test. It consists of two papers: Paper 1 (General Awareness) and Paper 2 (specific to the religious denomination). You must score minimum qualifying marks in each paper.

Step 3: Interview

Candidates who pass the written exam are interviewed by a board of officers. The interview assesses your religious knowledge, communication skills, confidence, and suitability for the role of a spiritual guide in the army.

Final Selection & Training

A final merit list is prepared based on the combined marks of the Written Exam and Interview. Selected candidates undergo a rigorous training program before being commissioned.

  • Training Academy: Institute of National Integration (INI), Pune.
  • Training Period: A specified duration of military and specialized religious training.
  • Focus: Basic military skills, army ethos, and honing their abilities as spiritual guides.

Religious Rituals üõê

Conducting prayers, religious ceremonies, and festivals according to the traditions of your faith. This is vital for maintaining the spiritual health and traditions within the unit.

Example: For a Hindu unit, you would conduct daily morning and evening aartis, organize pujas for festivals like Diwali and Holi, and perform samskaras (like naming ceremonies for children of soldiers) as required.

Spiritual Counseling ü§ù

Providing one-on-one counseling to soldiers facing personal, moral, or ethical dilemmas. You act as a trusted confidant and guide, helping them navigate stress and hardship.

Example: A young soldier is feeling immense stress due to family problems back home. You would provide a patient hearing, offer guidance based on religious teachings and scriptures, and help him find spiritual strength to cope.

Welfare of Troops ‚ù§Ô∏è

Visiting sick or injured soldiers in hospitals, writing to families in times of distress, and being a pillar of support for both the soldiers and their loved ones.

Example: When a soldier is admitted to the hospital, you visit him regularly to pray for his speedy recovery and boost his morale. If there's a casualty, you console the family and help them with the final rites as per their faith.

Moral Educator üìö

Delivering sermons and lectures on morality, ethics, and values. You are responsible for character development and instilling a strong sense of duty and righteousness in the troops.

Example: During a 'sainik sammelan', you deliver a talk on the importance of integrity and selfless service, drawing parallels from religious epics and teachings to inspire the troops.
