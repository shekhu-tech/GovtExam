Physical and Medical Standards üìä

Candidates must meet strict physical and medical standards set for military service:

Application - Offline/Online

Candidates must fill out the application form as specified in the advertisement and send it with required documents to the designated address.

Check Eligibility

Thoroughly check age, medical qualification, and other criteria.

Required Documents

Compile attested copies of all educational and registration certificates.

Shortlisting for Interview

Applications are scrutinized, and candidates are shortlisted based on their qualifications, experience, and the vacancies available in their specialty.

Interview Call-up Letter

Shortlisted candidates receive a call-up letter for the interview.

The Interview Board

The interview is conducted by a board of senior officers, including high-ranking AMC officers and specialists, to assess your professional knowledge and suitability for service.

Medical Examination

After the interview, recommended candidates undergo a detailed medical examination by a board of military doctors to ensure they meet the required medical standards for commissioning.

Physical Standards

Height, weight, and general physical fitness.

Medical Standards

Eyesight, hearing, and overall health assessment.

Eligibility Criteria

Example: A 42-year-old doctor with an MD in Anesthesiology, registered with the State Medical Council, and meeting the physical fitness norms is eligible to apply for the AMC specialist entry.

Required Documents

Example: You will need attested photocopies of your MBBS degree, PG Degree/Diploma certificate, NMC/State registration certificate, internship completion certificate, and birth certificate/matriculation certificate for age proof.

Call-up Letter

Example: You will receive a formal call-up letter detailing the date, time, and venue for the interview, typically at Army Hospital (Research & Referral) in Delhi, along with instructions for travel and required original documents.

Physical Standards

Example: A male candidate must have a minimum height of 157.5 cm, and a female candidate 152 cm. Weight should be proportionate to height, and there should be no skeletal deformities.

Medical Standards

Example: A candidate must not have conditions like cardiovascular disease, uncontrolled hypertension, or any active infectious disease. Vision standards may allow for corrective lenses up to a certain power, depending on the specialty.

Final Selection and Training

Selected candidates receive their appointment letters and are required to join for a basic military orientation course.

  • Training Academy: AMC Centre & College, Lucknow.
  • Training Period: A short orientation course (Medical Officers Basic Course - MOBC).
  • Focus: Military orientation, combat medical support, and adaptation to military life.

Example: An Orthopedic Surgeon selected for the AMC will first report to Lucknow for the MOBC, where they will learn about military protocols and their role in a field hospital before being posted to a service hospital.

Career Growth Path of an AMC Officer

Captain

Initial rank on commissioning for specialists. You are posted to a service hospital as a specialist.

Major

Promoted after about 4-5 years of service. You handle more complex cases and mentor junior doctors.

Lieutenant Colonel

Promoted after about 13 years of service. You may head your specialty department in a hospital.

Colonel

Promoted by selection after 20+ years. You could command a military hospital or hold senior staff appointments.

Brigadier / Major General

Command larger hospital setups (Command/R&R Hospitals) or hold senior administrative roles at Command HQ.

Lt. General (DGMS)

The pinnacle of the career, heading the entire Army Medical Corps as the Director General Medical Services (Army).

Clinical Specialist üè•

Example: As a specialist surgeon, your primary role will be to perform complex surgeries and manage patients in your field at well-equipped military hospitals across the country, from Pune to Udhampur.

Field Medical Support ‚ûï

Example: During exercises or operations, you may be deployed to forward areas to set up and run specialist facilities in field hospitals, providing critical medical care close to the front lines.

Hospital Administration üìã

Example: As you gain seniority, you will take on administrative roles. A senior cardiologist might be appointed the Head of Department, managing resources, personnel, and training for the cardiology wing of a Command Hospital.

Research & Academics üî¨

Example: You will have the opportunity to engage in research at premier institutions like AFMC Pune or Army Hospital (R&R) Delhi, and contribute to academic journals. You can also become a professor, teaching postgraduate medical students.
