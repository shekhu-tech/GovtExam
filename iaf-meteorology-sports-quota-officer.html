5. Training: Honing Specialised Skills ‚úàÔ∏è

Training is tailored to the role. Meteorology officers undergo officer training while sportspersons are trained as airmen.

1. For Meteorology Officers

Training is conducted at the Air Force Academy (AFA), Dundigal, followed by professional training.

  • Basic military and leadership training.
  • Rigorous academic curriculum on meteorology and atmospheric sciences.
  • Commissioned as a Flying Officer.

2. For Sportspersons

Undergo Joint Basic Phase Training (JBPT) at BTI, Belagavi, like other airmen.

  • Basic military discipline and fitness.
  • Allotted a suitable non-technical trade.
  • Provided with facilities and encouragement to pursue their sport.

6. Roles & Responsibilities üåü

These specialised personnel contribute to the IAF's mission through their unique expertise.

Meteorology Officer

  • Provide accurate weather forecasts for all flying operations.
  • Interpret satellite imagery and meteorological data.
  • Brief aircrew on weather conditions.
  • Act as the eyes and ears of the IAF in the atmospheric domain.

Sportsperson

  • Represent the Indian Air Force at national and international sports events.
  • Bring laurels to the service and the nation.
  • Inspire a culture of fitness and sportsmanship.
  • Perform duties of their allotted trade alongside sports.

Stage 1: Application Scrutiny

Applications are scrutinized based on the eligibility criteria. For Sports Quota, the level of participation and achievements are checked. For Meteorology, academic qualifications are verified. Shortlisted candidates are then called for the next phase.

Stage 2: Specialised Selection Tests

**For Sportspersons:** This involves physical fitness tests and comprehensive skill trials in their specific sport. **For Meteorology Candidates:** This is the full 5-day Air Force Selection Board (AFSB) process, which assesses officer-like qualities.

Stage 3: Medical Examination

Candidates recommended after the trials or AFSB interview undergo a detailed medical examination at a designated military medical centre to ensure they meet the required health and fitness standards for the Indian Air Force.
