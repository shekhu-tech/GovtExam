The Indian Air Force conducts **Recruitment Rallies** in various states and union territories across the country to select unmarried male candidates for enlistment as **Airmen in Group 'X' (Technical) and Group 'Y' (Non-Technical) Trades**. The selection tests are designed to assess your academic knowledge, physical prowess, and ability to adapt to the military environment.

  • Test-II (GD): For communication & teamwork.

8. Your Preparation Strategy: Smart Work üìà

Success demands a balanced approach, focusing equally on physical conditioning, academic preparation, and mental readiness.

üìö Preparation Steps:

1

Build Physical Stamina

Start running and exercising at least a month before the rally. Focus on meeting the PFT standards comfortably. Consistency is key.

2

Master the Syllabus

Thoroughly revise your 10+2 syllabus for Maths, Physics, and English. For RAGA, focus on basic reasoning, aptitude, and current affairs.

3

Practice Adaptability Tests

Practice Situation Reaction Tests from online sources. For GD, read newspapers and form opinions on common topics. Practice speaking in a group.

üîÑ Practice and Self-Assessment:

  • Time Management: Solve previous years' papers or mock tests within the time limit to improve speed and accuracy.
  • Group Discussions: If possible, practice group discussions with friends to build confidence and communication skills.
  • Stay Informed: Regularly follow the news and current events to perform well in the General Awareness part of the RAGA section.

9. When do applications open? üóìÔ∏è

IAF Rally notifications are not on a fixed annual calendar; they are released based on the recruitment needs of the Air Force. Aspirants must remain vigilant and check official sources regularly.

1

Official Notification

Source: Rally advertisements are published on the official Airmen Selection website (airmenselection.cdac.in) and in leading national/local newspapers, including Employment News.

2

Pre-Registration Period

Process: Some rallies may require online pre-registration. The notification will clearly state the window for registration, which is typically open for a short period.

3

Rally Dates

Schedule: The notification specifies the exact dates and venue for the rally, usually covering several districts over a period of 4-6 days.

10. What Not to Do? üö´

To ensure a fair and successful attempt, it is crucial to avoid common pitfalls and prohibited activities.

‚ùå
Don't fall for touts: The selection process is purely merit-based. Do not pay money to anyone promising selection. Report such persons to the authorities.
‚ùå
Don't hide medical facts: Be completely honest during the medical examination. Hiding any past injuries or current ailments can lead to permanent disqualification.
‚ùå
Don't carry prohibited items: Mobile phones, smart watches, and any electronic gadgets are strictly banned at the rally and examination venues.
‚ùå
Don't use performance enhancers: Candidates found to have used any performance-enhancing drugs during the PFT will be debarred from the selection process.
