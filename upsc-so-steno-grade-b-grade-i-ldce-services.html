8. Preparation Strategy üìà

Master CSMOP (Manual of Office Procedure)

This is the bible for SO/Steno exam. Study all chapters thoroughly, especially noting and drafting procedures.

Focus on Paper II (Noting & Drafting)

Practice daily. Most candidates fail here despite knowing rules. Write at least one noting daily.

Previous Years' Questions

Solve at least 5 years PYQs to understand pattern and frequently asked topics.

Service Rules Thoroughly

FR/SR, CCS (Conduct) Rules, CCS (CCA) Rules, Pension Rules must be memorized with exact rules.

Section Officer (SO)

Primary Role:

Manage sections in Central Ministries, handle files, draft policies, ensure smooth administration.

Key Responsibilities:

File processing, noting & drafting, coordination between levels, policy implementation, supervision of staff.

Career: Gazetted Officer (Group B), first level of officer rank in Secretariat.

Stenographer (Grade B/I)

Primary Role:

Assist senior officers (Joint Secretary & above) with dictation, confidential documents, scheduling.

Key Skills:

Shorthand (120 wpm), typing (40 wpm), confidentiality, office management, communication.

Note: Separate shorthand test for Steno candidates in addition to written papers.

Stage 1: Written Exam

Three Papers held on different days. Total 300 Marks.

Key Details:

  • Paper I: General Studies & Constitution (100 Marks, 2 Hours)
  • Paper II: Noting, Drafting & Precis (100 Marks, 3 Hours)
  • Paper III: Service Rules (100 Marks, 3 Hours)
  • Negative Marking: 1/3rd for wrong answers in Paper I only
  • ‚Ä¢ Weightage: 75% of total (300/400)
  • ‚Ä¢ Qualifying marks decided by UPSC

Tip: Time management in Paper II is crucial - 3 hours for noting, drafting & precis.

Stage 2: Service Record Evaluation

100 Marks | Decides final ranking

  • ‚Ä¢ APAR/ACR Grading: Last 5 years (60 Marks)
  • ‚Ä¢ Educational Qualifications: Additional degrees (10 Marks)
  • ‚Ä¢ Departmental Exams: Previous performance (10 Marks)
  • ‚Ä¢ Training Courses: Completed (10 Marks)
  • ‚Ä¢ Service Experience: Years of service (10 Marks)
  • ‚Ä¢ Important: Clean record with no penalties is mandatory

Tip: Maintain excellent APAR grading throughout service.

Paper I: GS & Constitution

Objective Type | 100 Marks

Syllabus:

  • Indian Constitution: Preamble, Fundamental Rights, DPSP, Parliament, Judiciary
  • Parliamentary Procedure: Question Hour, Zero Hour, Committees, Legislation process
  • Current Affairs: National & International events of last 1 year
  • Economic Development: Five Year Plans, Budget, Economic surveys
  • General Science: Basic concepts, Recent developments
  • Geography & History: Indian geography, Freedom movement

Current Affairs carries about 30 marks - stay updated!

Paper II: Noting & Drafting

Descriptive Type | 100 Marks

Exam Pattern:

  • Noting on Files: 2 questions (25 marks each) - Prepare notes on given situations
  • Drafting Official Letters: 2 questions (15 marks each) - Draft letters/OMs/notifications
  • Precis Writing: 1 passage (20 marks) - Reduce to 1/3rd length

Key Areas:

CSMOP formats, Government of India (Transaction of Business) Rules, Proper file noting procedure, Precise language, Clear recommendations.

Practice daily - this paper is the make or break!

Paper III: Service Rules

Descriptive Type | 100 Marks

Syllabus:

  • FR/SR (Fundamental Rules & Supplementary Rules): Pay, Leave, Pension, Travel rules
  • CCS (Conduct) Rules, 1964: Conduct of govt servants, Political activities
  • CCS (CCA) Rules, 1965: Disciplinary proceedings, Penalties
  • CCS (Pension) Rules, 1972: Retirement benefits, Family pension
  • General Financial Rules (GFR): Government expenditure, Tenders, Contracts
  • RTI Act, 2005: Provisions related to govt offices

Exact rule numbers and wordings are important!

SO/Steno Career Path

1. Section Officer / Steno Gr B

Entry via LDCE. First Gazetted Officer level.
Group B Gazetted. Section in-charge.

2. Under Secretary / PPS

Next promotion (approx 5-6 yrs). Under Secretary in Ministry or Principal Private Secretary to senior officers.

3. Deputy Secretary / Director

Senior management role. Heads division in Ministry. Significant policy role.

4. Joint Secretary

Apex level in Central Secretariat. Heads wing in Ministry. Policy formulation level.

5. Additional Secretary -> Secretary

Highest bureaucratic positions. Secretary to Government of India heads entire Ministry.

Promotions based on seniority, departmental exams, and performance.
