5. Training: Forging a Soldier ‚öîÔ∏è

Selected recruits undergo rigorous Basic Military Training (BMT) followed by Advanced & Specialised Training at various Regimental Centres.

1. Basic Military Training (BMT)

This phase focuses on turning a civilian into a disciplined, physically tough, and mentally robust soldier.

  • Physical conditioning and obstacle courses.
  • Drill, turnout, and military bearing.
  • Weapon handling, firing, and field craft.

2. Advanced Training

After BMT, recruits are sent to their respective centres (e.g., Armoured Corps Centre, Artillery Centre) for trade-specific training.

  • Specialised technical and tactical training.
  • Learning to operate specific weapons and equipment.
  • Becoming a proficient member of their unit.

8. Preparation Strategy: Train to Win üìà

A disciplined and balanced approach focusing on both physical and academic preparation is the key to success.

üìö Key Steps:

  1. Conquer the PFT

    Focus on clearing the 1.6km run well within the Group 1 timing (5m 30s) and practicing pull-ups to maximize marks.

  2. Master the CEE Syllabus

    Prepare for the Common Entrance Exam based on the syllabus for your chosen trade. Regular practice is essential.

  3. Get Medically Checked

    Undergo a preliminary medical check-up to identify and rectify minor issues like dental points, flat foot, or knocking knees beforehand.

üîÑ Habits & Practice:

  • Balanced Diet: Fuel your body for intense physical training with a nutritious diet.
  • Time Management: Practice solving CEE sample papers under timed conditions.
  • Documentation: Prepare all required documents, including domicile, caste, and educational certificates, well in advance.

Phase 1: Online Common Entrance Exam (CEE)

The first phase is a computer-based test conducted at designated centers. The exam pattern and syllabus differ based on the category (GD, Technical, Clerk, etc.). Candidates must clear this exam to be shortlisted for Phase 2.

Phase 2: Recruitment Rally

Shortlisted candidates are called to the rally site. Here, their documents are verified, followed by a Physical Fitness Test (PFT) and Physical Measurement Test (PMT). The PFT includes a 1.6 km run, pull-ups, 9 feet ditch jump, and zig-zag balance.

Stage 3: Medical Examination

Candidates who clear the Phase 2 rally tests undergo a detailed medical examination by military doctors to ensure they meet the stringent medical standards of the Indian Army.
