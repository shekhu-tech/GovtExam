5. Mandatory Papers of Mains: Structure and Importance üìù

The NPSC Mains exam has a total of 6 papers, all compulsory and descriptive.

English and Language (Paper I & II)

These papers test your command over the English and Nagamese languages. Each is of 100 marks.

GS Papers (3 Papers, 100-100 Marks)

These include subjects like history, geography, polity, economy, science, and technology of Nagaland and India. Each paper is of 100 marks.

Optional Subject (2 Papers, 100-100 Marks)

This paper is to test the expertise of the candidate.

6. Importance of Nagaland-Centric Subjects üåü

In the NPSC exam, questions related to Nagaland's history, geography, politics, and economy are very important.

History and Culture

Special attention is given to the history of Nagaland, tribal culture, folk art, music, literature, and major religious and tourist places.

Geography and Economy

Questions are asked about the geography of Nagaland, its forests, rivers, minerals, agriculture, and key government development projects.

üí° Why are these subjects important?

A good grasp of these subjects is very important for final selection as they show your understanding and awareness of the state.

Prelims (Preliminary Exam) - Objective

This is a screening test with two papers. The merit is based on Paper-I only.

GS Paper I (150 Marks)

150 questions, 2 hours. The merit is based on this. There is a 1/3 negative marking.

GS Paper II (150 Marks)

150 questions, 2 hours. This is also counted for merit. There is a 1/3 negative marking.

Mains (Main Exam) - Written (Total 1200 Marks)

It has a total of 6 papers, all compulsory and descriptive.

English (100 Marks)

Essay, letter writing and grammar.

Nagamese (100 Marks)

Nagamese language skills.

GS I (100 Marks)

History and Geography.

GS II (100 Marks)

Indian Polity and Public Administration.

GS III (100 Marks)

Science and Technology.

GS IV (200 Marks)

Ethics and General Aptitude.

Interview (Personality Test)

This is the final stage of the NPSC Civil Services exam. It is a verbal test of about 20-30 minutes.

  • Nature: Verbal test - assessment of personality.
  • Objective: To evaluate the candidate's mental qualities, administrative ability, and understanding of issues related to Nagaland.
  • Marks: 150 marks.
  • The final merit list is prepared based on the total **1350 marks** of Mains (1200 marks) and Interview (150 marks).
