5. Training & Probation üèõÔ∏è

Selected Development Assistants undergo a probation period to learn the bank's procedures, while Research Officers are engaged for a specific project duration.

1. Development Assistant

Probation is focused on administrative training.

  • A probation period of six months.
  • Induction training on NABARD's functions, rules, and office procedures.
  • Confirmation is subject to satisfactory performance.

2. Research Officer

This is a contractual role tied to a project.

  • Engaged for a fixed term as per the project requirements.
  • No probation period in the traditional sense.
  • Performance is linked to the delivery of project objectives and reports.

6. Key Responsibilities üåü

The responsibilities are distinct, with Development Assistants providing crucial clerical support and Research Officers providing expert analytical input.

Development Assistant

  • Maintaining files and records.
  • Data entry and preparing statements.
  • Handling day-to-day correspondence.
  • Assisting officers in their administrative work.

DA (Hindi)

  • Translating documents from English to Hindi and vice-versa.
  • Assisting in the implementation of the Official Language Policy.
  • Preparing reports and correspondence in Hindi.

Research Officer

  • Conducting primary and secondary research.
  • Data analysis using statistical software.
  • Writing high-quality research papers and policy briefs.

DA Prelims Exam

This is a qualifying online exam of 100 marks, with a composite time of 60 minutes. It consists of three sections: English, Numerical Ability, and Reasoning. Marks are not counted for the final merit, but candidates must clear the cut-off to be shortlisted for Phase II.

DA Mains Exam

The Mains exam is the merit-deciding stage. It includes objective tests on Reasoning, Quantitative Aptitude, General Awareness, and Computer Knowledge, plus a Descriptive Test on English (Essay, Precis, Report/Letter Writing). For DA (Hindi), the test of professional knowledge (Hindi) is included.

RO Shortlisting

For Research Officer/Project Scientist roles, there is no written exam. Applications are directly shortlisted based on eligibility criteria, academic qualifications, research experience, publications, and the quality of the research proposal submitted.

RO Interview

Shortlisted candidates for Research Officer roles are called for a personal interview. This is the final selection stage where a panel of experts assesses the candidate's domain knowledge, research aptitude, and suitability for the specific project. There is no interview for the Development Assistant post.
