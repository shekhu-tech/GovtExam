5. Phase II Syllabus: Post-Specific üìã

Phase II exams are specific to the post you have applied for and test your in-depth knowledge of the relevant subjects. There are different papers for different categories.

AG-III (General & Depot)

Paper I: English, Reasoning, Numerical Aptitude, General Studies, Computer Awareness.

Paper II: Not applicable. Only Paper I for these posts.

AG-III (Accounts & Technical)

Paper I: Common for all posts (same as above).

Paper II: Specific to the post, e.g., Accounts (Commerce) and Technical (Agriculture, Botany, etc.)

6. Post-wise Details & Duties üåü

Understanding your role is crucial. Each post has a distinct set of responsibilities within the Food Corporation of India.

AG-III (General/Depot)

  • General administration duties.
  • Managing food grain stocks.
  • Record keeping and reporting.
  • Clerical work at godowns and offices.

AG-III (Accounts)

  • Maintaining financial records.
  • Accounting and auditing tasks.
  • Handling invoices and payments.
  • Preparing financial statements.

AG-III (Technical)

  • Quality control of food grains.
  • Ensuring proper storage and preservation.
  • Technical inspections and checks.
  • Pest control measures.

8. Your Preparation Strategy üìà

Success in the FCI exam requires a structured and consistent approach. Proper planning and dedicated practice are the keys to cracking the exam.

üìö Key Steps:

  1. Understand the Syllabus

    Thoroughly go through the official syllabus and exam pattern for both Phase I and Phase II papers.

  2. Create a Study Plan

    Allocate time for each subject based on your strengths and weaknesses. Focus more on areas where you need improvement.

  3. Practice with Mock Tests

    Regularly take full-length mock tests to get familiar with the exam environment and improve time management.

üîÑ Habits & Practice:

  • Daily Reading: Read newspapers and magazines to stay updated on current affairs and improve English.
  • Solve Previous Year Papers: This gives you a clear idea of the type of questions asked and the difficulty level.
  • Focus on Accuracy: With negative marking, it is crucial to maintain accuracy. Don't rush into answering.
  • Revision: Regularly revise what you have studied. This helps in long-term retention of information.

Phase I Online Exam

This is a common objective-type exam for all posts. It acts as a screening test. The paper consists of 100 questions to be completed in 60 minutes. There is a negative marking of 0.25 for each wrong answer.

Phase II Online Exam

The Phase II exam is more specific and tests the in-depth knowledge required for the applied post. The exam structure varies depending on the category (General, Accounts, Technical, etc.).

Document Verification

Candidates who qualify in the online exams are called for document verification. This is a crucial step where your eligibility, as claimed in the application, is verified with original documents.
