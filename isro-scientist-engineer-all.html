1. What is the Scientist/Engineer 'SC' Role? üí°

The Scientist/Engineer 'SC' is the entry-level post for engineers in the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). It is a prestigious Group 'A' Gazetted post. Scientists/Engineers are the masterminds behind India's space missions, responsible for the design, development, and operation of launch vehicles (rockets), satellites, and their associated ground systems.

5. Training & Induction üåå

Newly recruited Scientist/Engineers undergo a one-year induction training program at one of the ISRO centres.

Provides exposure to the various programmes and activities of the Department of Space.

Stage 1: Written Test

The first stage is a comprehensive Computer Based Test (CBT). It consists of objective questions from the core engineering discipline syllabus and may also include an aptitude section. A high score in this test is necessary to be shortlisted for the interview.

Stage 2: Personal Interview

Shortlisted candidates are called for a personal interview, which is highly technical in nature. The interview panel, consisting of senior ISRO scientists, assesses the candidate's fundamental knowledge, problem-solving skills, and passion for a research career.

Final Selection

The final merit list is prepared based on the combined weightage of marks from the Written Test and the Personal Interview (typically 50% each). Selected candidates then undergo document verification and medical examination before receiving their appointment letters.
