5. Training & Probation üèõÔ∏è

BHEL places a strong emphasis on training to develop a highly skilled workforce. All new entrants go through a structured induction and training program.

1. Training Period

A one-year training period is standard for all trainee positions.

  • Induction program covering company vision, values, and business operations.
  • Classroom training on technical and managerial subjects.
  • Extensive on-the-job training at BHEL's manufacturing units and power project sites across India.

2. Probation & Absorption

After training, performance is evaluated for regularisation.

  • Upon successful completion of training, trainees are absorbed into the company's regular rolls.
  • Engineer/Executive Trainees are absorbed in the E1 grade.
  • They are placed on probation for a period of one year following absorption.

8. Your Preparation Strategy üìà

Preparation should be sharply focused on the mode of entry: acing the GATE exam for Engineer Trainees or cracking BHEL's own competitive test for other trainee roles.

üìö For Engineer Trainees (GATE):

  1. Target a Top GATE Rank

    Your entire focus should be on securing a very high score and rank in your respective GATE paper (ME, EE, ECE, etc.) as the competition is extremely high.

  2. Prepare for GD & Interview

    After securing a good GATE score, prepare for the Group Discussion and Personal Interview. Revise core technical subjects and be aware of BHEL's business profile.

üîÑ For Supervisor/Executive Trainees:

  • Master Your Domain: The technical/professional knowledge section has the highest weightage. Thoroughly cover your entire Diploma or Degree syllabus.
  • Practice Aptitude: The general aptitude section is equally important for clearing the cut-off. Practice regularly to build speed and accuracy.
  • Solve Previous Papers: Practice with previous years' papers of BHEL and other PSUs to get accustomed to the pattern.

9. Recruitment Cycle & Process üóìÔ∏è

BHEL's Engineer Trainee recruitment is aligned with the GATE examination cycle. Other vacancies are advertised based on organizational needs.

Notification Release

Timeline: ET notifications are published on the BHEL Careers portal (careers.bhel.in) after GATE results. Other notifications are posted as and when vacancies arise.

Application & Screening

Timeline: Candidates apply online. Shortlisting is done based on GATE scores for ETs and on written test performance for other roles.

GD & Interview

Timeline: Shortlisted candidates are invited for Group Discussion and Personal Interviews.

Final Result

Timeline: A final merit list is created with weightage given to GATE Score/Written Test, GD, and Interview marks. Offer letters are issued after a medical examination.

Stage 1: Initial Screening

The first step for shortlisting candidates. For Engineer Trainees, this is based on the normalized marks obtained in the Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering (GATE). For other roles like Supervisor Trainee, BHEL conducts its own Computer Based Test (CBT) which has both general aptitude and technical sections.

Stage 2: Group Discussion

Candidates shortlisted from Stage 1 (based on GATE score or written test marks) are invited for a Group Discussion (GD). The GD is designed to assess a candidate's communication skills, leadership potential, teamwork, and analytical abilities on a given topic.

Stage 3: Personal Interview

This is the final stage of the selection process. A panel of senior BHEL executives interviews the candidates to assess their technical knowledge, behavioral skills, and overall suitability for a career in BHEL. The final merit list is prepared with specific weightages assigned to the screening score, GD, and Interview.
