Specialist Services Through IES/ISS

1. Indian Economic Service (IES)

Involved in economic analysis, policy formulation, and implementation. IES officers work in Ministry of Finance, NITI Aayog, Ministry of Commerce, and other economic departments.

Roles: Economic analysis, policy formulation, budget preparation

2. Indian Statistical Service (ISS)

Responsible for India's official statistical system. ISS officers work on design of surveys, data collection, analysis, and dissemination in National Statistical Office (NSO).

Roles: Data collection, statistical analysis, survey design

Stage 1: Written Exam

Descriptive (Subjective) type offline examination. No negative marking.

Total: 1000 Marks

  • Paper I & II: General English & General Studies (200 Marks)
  • Paper III-VI: Subject Specific Papers (800 Marks)
  • â€¢ Each paper is 3 hours duration
  • â€¢ Descriptive/Subjective type answers

Note: No negative marking. Quality of answer writing is crucial.

Stage 2: Interview

Candidates who clear the written exam are called for interview at UPSC, New Delhi.

  • 1.Marks: 200 Marks (added to written score)
  • 2.Duration: 30-40 minutes per candidate
  • 3.Assessment: Subject knowledge, analytical ability, personality, communication skills
  • 4.Focus: Current economic affairs, statistical applications, policy issues
  • 5.Final Merit: Written (1000) + Interview (200) = 1200 Marks

Tip: Be updated with recent economic policies and statistical developments.

Indian Economic Service (IES)

IES officers are involved in economic analysis, policy formulation, and implementation at the highest levels of government. They work in key ministries like Finance, Commerce, NITI Aayog, and contribute to major policy documents like the Economic Survey and Union Budget.

Example: An IES officer might analyze inflation trends to recommend monetary policy actions, or evaluate the impact of a new trade agreement on India's economy.

Indian Statistical Service (ISS)

ISS officers are the backbone of India's statistical system. They design and implement large-scale surveys like the NSS, analyze economic and social data, and provide statistical inputs for policy formulation. They primarily work in the National Statistical Office (NSO) and other data-intensive departments.

Example: An ISS officer might design the sampling methodology for the Periodic Labour Force Survey (PLFS) or calculate GDP growth estimates for the current quarter.
