5. Training & Probation üèõÔ∏è

Newly appointed employees are confirmed in their posts after successfully completing a mandatory probation period.

1. Probation Period

All new recruits are initially on probation.

  • The standard probation period in Arunachal Pradesh is two years.
  • Performance and conduct are evaluated for confirmation in the service.

2. On-the-Job Training

Focuses on departmental rules and procedures.

  • Induction on state government service rules.
  • Practical training on the specific duties of the post, including file management and public dealing.

6. Key Responsibilities üåü

These roles are diverse and integral to the functioning of public services across the districts of Arunachal Pradesh.

Admin & Revenue

  • **Clerk/DEO:** Administrative duties in DC offices.
  • **Amin/Patwari:** Land record and survey work.
  • **Court Staff:** Supporting the district judiciary.

Public Welfare

  • **Health Dept:** ANM, GNM, Lab Techs in health centers.
  • **Education Dept:** Guest Teachers and Resource Persons.
  • **ICDS:** Anganwadi Workers and Helpers.

Local Governance

  • **Panchayat Sahayak:** Assisting Gram Panchayats.
  • **Municipal Staff:** Providing civic services in towns.
  • **DRDA Staff:** Implementing rural development schemes.

8. Your Preparation Strategy üìà

A successful preparation strategy involves a thorough understanding of the APSSB syllabus and dedicated practice.

üìö Key Steps:

  1. Cover the Syllabus

    Focus on the three core subjects: General English, Elementary Maths, and General Knowledge. Use NCERT books up to Class 10 as a base.

  2. Focus on Arunachal GK

    Pay special attention to the state's history, tribes, geography, and current affairs, as this is a key part of the GK section.

  3. Practice Skill Tests

    For LDC/Steno posts, daily typing or stenography practice is essential to qualify the skill test stage.

üîÑ Habits & Practice:

  • Solve APSSB Previous Papers: The best way to understand the exam pattern and difficulty level.
  • Read Newspapers: To stay updated on national and state-level current affairs.
  • Regular Mock Tests: To build speed and accuracy for the written examination.

9. Recruitment Cycle & Process üóìÔ∏è

Recruitment is conducted by the Arunachal Pradesh Staff Selection Board (APSSB) whenever vacancies are reported by various government departments.

APSSB Notification

Timeline: Vacancies are advertised on the official APSSB website (apssb.nic.in).

Written Examination

Timeline: A written test (OMR-based or online) is conducted a few months after the application process.

Skill Test & Document Verification

Timeline: Shortlisted candidates are called for the skill test (if required) and document verification.

Final Appointment

Timeline: A final merit list is prepared based on written test scores, and appointment letters are issued by the concerned departments.
