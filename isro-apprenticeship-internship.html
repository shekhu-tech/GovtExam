5. Program Structure & Benefits ‚úàÔ∏è

Both programs offer a monthly stipend and invaluable practical experience.

1. Apprenticeship

A one-year training program under the Apprentices Act, 1961.

  • Fixed monthly stipend (varies for ITI, Diploma, Graduates).
  • Training in a specific trade/discipline.
  • Certificate of proficiency upon completion.
  • No guarantee of employment in DRDO.

2. Internship

A short-term project-based engagement for current students.

  • Duration varies from 1 to 6 months.
  • Work on a live DRDO project under a scientist's guidance.
  • May offer a stipend and certificate.
  • Excellent for fulfilling academic requirements.

Stage 1: Application

Candidates must apply as per the procedure mentioned in the advertisement. For Apprenticeships, this often involves applying through the NATS portal. For Internships, applications may be sent directly to the lab or through an online portal.

Stage 2: Shortlisting

Applications are scrutinized, and candidates are shortlisted purely based on the marks obtained in their qualifying examinations (ITI, Diploma, B.E./B.Tech, B.Sc, etc.). A merit list is prepared for this purpose.

Stage 3: Verification and Selection

Shortlisted candidates are called for document verification. Some labs may conduct a brief interview to assess the candidate's interest and basic knowledge. Final selection is confirmed after this stage.
