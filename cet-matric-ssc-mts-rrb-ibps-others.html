1. What is NRA CET? üí°

The National Recruitment Agency (NRA) has been set up to conduct a Common Eligibility Test (CET) to screen candidates for various non-gazetted Group 'B' and 'C' posts in the Central Government. This single online exam aims to replace the multiple preliminary exams conducted by agencies like the Staff Selection Commission (SSC), Railway Recruitment Boards (RRBs), and the Institute of Banking Personnel Selection (IBPS), streamlining the selection process for millions of aspirants.

NRA will conduct separate CETs for three levels: Matriculate (10th), Higher Secondary (12th), and Graduate.

  • üéÇ
    Age Limit: The age limits will be as per the existing requirements of the recruiting agencies for specific posts (e.g., 18-25 for MTS, 18-33 for Group D). Age relaxations will apply as per rules.
  • üéì
    Educational Qualification: The minimum qualification will be Matriculation (10th Pass) for the Matric Level CET, which will cover posts like SSC MTS and RRB Group D.

CET Score Validity & Attempts üìä

  • The CET score will be valid for **3 years** from the date of declaration of the result.
  • There will be **no restriction** on the number of attempts, subject to the upper age limit.
  • The best of the valid scores shall be deemed to be the candidate's current score.

5. Post-Selection Training üèõÔ∏è

After final selection by the respective agency (SSC, RRB, IBPS), new recruits undergo a standard probation and training period.

1. Probation Period

All new government employees are placed on probation.

  • A probation period of two years is standard.
  • Performance, conduct, and diligence are monitored.
  • Permanent appointment is confirmed after successful completion of probation.

2. Induction Training

Training is provided to familiarize recruits with their duties.

  • Orientation on government service rules and conduct.
  • Specific training related to the job profile (e.g., banking procedures for clerks, railway operations for Group D).

8. Your Preparation Strategy üìà

The key to cracking the NRA CET is consistent practice and building speed across all sections of the common syllabus.

üìö Key Steps:

  1. Strengthen the Basics

    Focus on clearing your fundamental concepts in Maths, Reasoning, and English from standard school-level books (Class 8-10).

  2. Practice Extensively

    Solve as many practice questions as possible for each topic to build both speed and accuracy.

  3. Take Mock Tests

    Regularly attempt full-length mock tests to get used to the exam pattern, manage your time effectively, and identify weak areas.

üîÑ Habits & Practice:

  • Daily Newspaper Reading: Helps improve English comprehension and keeps you updated with General Knowledge.
  • Consistent Revision: Make short notes of important formulas and concepts and revise them regularly.
  • Analyze Mocks: After each mock test, spend time analyzing your mistakes to avoid repeating them.
  • Stay Informed: Keep track of official announcements from NRA regarding the syllabus and exam dates.

Stage 1: NRA Common Eligibility Test (CET)

This will be the first stage of screening for all participating posts. It will be a single online objective test covering subjects like Reasoning, Quant, English, and General Awareness. The score of this test will be valid for 3 years.

Stage 2: Mains Examination

Candidates who qualify the CET with a valid score can apply for the Mains (Tier-II/III) examination for specific posts, which will be conducted by the respective agencies like SSC, RRB, or IBPS. The syllabus and pattern of the Mains exam will be set by these agencies.

Stage 3: Skill Test / Physical Test

For certain posts, there may be a final qualifying stage. For example, a typing test for clerical posts or a Physical Efficiency Test (PET) for certain Group D posts in the railways. This stage is typically qualifying in nature and is conducted by the final recruiting agency.
