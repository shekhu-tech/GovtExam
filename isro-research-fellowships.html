1. About ISRO Research Fellowships 🌌

ISRO offers prestigious research fellowships—Junior Research Fellow (JRF), Senior Research Fellow (SRF), and Research Associate (RA)—to bright young minds. These positions allow you to work on cutting-edge space science and technology projects, contributing directly to India's space program while pursuing advanced research.

3. Eligibility Blueprint ✅

Strict academic criteria must be met to apply for these research positions:

  • 🎓
    Education: **M.Sc/M.E./M.Tech** for JRF/SRF, and **Ph.D.** for RA in relevant disciplines. A high aggregate score is mandatory.
  • GATE NET CSIR:">📝
    Qualifying Exams: Candidates must have qualified **CSIR-UGC NET, GATE, JEST, or equivalent** national-level tests.
  • 🎂
    Age Limit: Typically **28 years for JRF, 32 for SRF, and 35 for RA**, with relaxations for reserved categories.

Experience & Attempts 📊

While JRF is an entry-level fellowship, SRF and RA positions require prior research experience.

Number of Attempts:

There is no limit on attempts. You can apply for any opening as long as you meet the age and qualification criteria specified in the notification.

9. Recruitment Cycle & Process 🗓️

10. Common Mistakes to Avoid 🚫

Avoid these common errors that can derail your chances of securing a prestigious ISRO fellowship.

❌
Superficial Subject Knowledge:

Relying on rote memorization instead of deep conceptual understanding.

❌
Unclear Research Interest:

Being unable to articulate why you want to do research in a specific area at ISRO.

❌
Ignoring Your Own Project:

Not being able to defend or explain the methodology and findings of your past research work.

❌
Lack of Professionalism:

Poor communication skills or a casual attitude during the interview.

Application Screening

Your application will be screened based on academic performance (CGPA/Percentage), qualification in national tests like NET/GATE, and research experience (for SRF/RA). Only highly qualified candidates are shortlisted.

Written Test & Interview

For JRF, a written test may be conducted, followed by an interview. For SRF and RA, selection is typically through an interview alone. The interview will be highly technical and focused on your subject area and past research.

Final Selection

A final merit list is prepared based on performance in the interview (and written test, if applicable). Selected candidates will receive an offer letter detailing the fellowship terms, stipend, and joining date.
