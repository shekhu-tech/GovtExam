The University Entry Scheme (UES) is a unique opportunity to join the Indian Armed Forces. It provides a chance for engineering and final year graduate students to become an officer in the **Indian Army, Navy, or Air Force** directly without a written exam.

5. Academic Requirements: Core and Soft Skills ‚úçÔ∏è

There is no traditional written exam for UES, but your academic record and some key skills are evaluated.

1. Core Engineering Knowledge

Your foundational knowledge of your engineering subject is tested in the campus interview and SSB.

  • Strong grasp of core subjects of your stream.
  • Combination of theoretical and practical knowledge.
  • Detailed understanding of your final year project.

2. Soft Skills and Personality

In the SSB, your leadership, team spirit, and communication skills are evaluated.

  • Good communication skills and ability to speak clearly.
  • Ability to work effectively in a group.
  • Confidence and stress management.

Eligibility Criteria

The first step is to ensure you meet the basic eligibility criteria for age, education, and nationality.

Example: A 22-year-old final-year B.Tech student in Computer Science is eligible to apply for UES, provided they are an Indian citizen.

Required Documents

You will need to submit a set of documents during the application and interview stages.

Example: Mark sheets from all semesters, a valid photo ID, and a recent passport-size photograph are some of the key documents required for the application process.

Technical Interview

This interview assesses your core technical knowledge. Questions will be based on your engineering or graduation subjects, and your final year project.

Example: A civil engineering candidate may be asked questions about different types of bridges, building materials, or the mechanics of structures.

General Interview

This part of the interview evaluates your personality, hobbies, and general awareness. It aims to understand your motivation, character, and communication skills.

Example: You might be asked about your hobbies, why you want to join the armed forces, or your thoughts on a recent national event.

Day 1: Screening Test

This is the first day of the SSB. It includes two tests: the Officer Intelligence Rating (OIR) test and the Picture Perception and Discussion Test (PPDT).

Example: In the PPDT, you are shown a blurry picture for 30 seconds and then have to write a story about it. Afterwards, you discuss your story with a group of other candidates.

Day 2: Psychological Test

This is a series of written tests designed to assess your mental state and emotional stability. It includes TAT, WAT, SRT, and SD.

Example: In the Word Association Test (WAT), you are shown a word for 15 seconds and have to write a sentence that comes to your mind first. This tests your subconscious thought process.

Day 3-4: GTO Test

The Group Testing Officer (GTO) test is a series of outdoor tasks that assess your social skills, leadership, and teamwork. It includes GD, GPE, PGT, and HGT.

Example: In the Progressive Group Task (PGT), your group has to cross a series of obstacles using planks and ropes, showcasing your ability to work with others to solve a problem.

Day 5: Conference

This is the final day where you have a brief interaction with all the assessors. They review your performance and make a final recommendation.

Example: You will sit in front of the board of officers and they may ask a few general questions like "How was your stay?" or "What did you learn from the process?"

Physical Standards

You must meet the minimum physical standards for height and weight. There are also specific requirements for physical fitness.

Example: A candidate for the Indian Army must be at least 157.5 cm tall and have a proportionate weight. Physical tests like running and push-ups are also part of the assessment.

Medical Standards

A thorough medical check-up is conducted to ensure you are free from any chronic diseases or physical deformities. This includes tests for vision, hearing, and dental health.

Example: A candidate with flat feet or a history of major surgery may be deemed medically unfit. Clear vision without glasses is a common requirement.
