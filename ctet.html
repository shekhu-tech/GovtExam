1. What is CTET? ü§î

The Central Teacher Eligibility Test (CTET) is conducted by the CBSE for candidates who wish to become teachers for Classes 1 to 8 in schools run by the Central Government. The CTET certificate is a mandatory qualification for teaching jobs in these schools.

5. Subject-Focused Preparation üß†

A tailored approach for each subject can help you master the concepts and score well.

Child Development & Pedagogy

Focus on key theories of child psychology (Piaget, Vygotsky), learning concepts, and classroom practices. This section has high weightage in both papers.

Language I & II

Practice unseen passages for comprehension and pay attention to language acquisition principles and grammar rules.

Mathematics & Science / Social Studies

Base your preparation on NCERT textbooks from classes 1-8. Focus on developing conceptual clarity rather than rote learning.

7. Revision and Mock Tests üîÑ

Revisiting concepts and practicing under timed conditions are critical for success.

  • Less is More: Rely on a few trusted sources and revise them thoroughly.
  • Solve PYQs: Solving Previous Year Question Papers (PYQs) helps you understand the exam pattern and important topics.
  • Mock Tests: Take regular mock tests to improve your speed and time management.

8. Exam Day Rules & Regulations üìã

Following the rules on the exam day ensures a smooth and stress-free experience.

  • Admit Card & ID: It is mandatory to carry your admit card and a valid photo ID. Without them, entry is not permitted.
  • Prohibited Items: Mobile phones, smartwatches, and any electronic devices are strictly prohibited inside the exam hall.
  • Time Management: Utilize the 15-minute reading time wisely. Manage your time to attempt all questions.

9. What Not to Do? üö´

Knowing which mistakes to avoid is just as crucial as knowing how to study.

10. Qualifying Marks üíØ

To pass the CTET exam, you need to score a minimum percentage of marks. There is no negative marking for incorrect answers.

General Category

You must score a minimum of 60% (90 out of 150 marks) to qualify.

Reserved Categories

Candidates from SC/ST/OBC/PwD categories need to score a minimum of 55% (82 out of 150 marks).

Paper I (Classes 1-5)

Paper I consists of 150 multiple-choice questions, with no negative marking. The duration is 2.5 hours.

  • Subjects: Child Development & Pedagogy, Language I, Language II, Mathematics, Environmental Studies (EVS).
  • Marks: Each subject has 30 questions, for a total of 150 marks.

Paper II (Classes 6-8)

Paper II also has 150 multiple-choice questions, with no negative marking. The duration is 2.5 hours.

  • Subjects: Child Development & Pedagogy, Language I, Language II, and either Mathematics & Science OR Social Studies.
  • Marks: Child Development & Pedagogy, Language I, and Language II have 30 questions each, while the subject-specific section has 60 questions. Total 150 marks.
