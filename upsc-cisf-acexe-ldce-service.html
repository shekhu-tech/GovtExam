Industrial Security

Primary Role:

Protect critical infrastructure including Power Plants, Oil Refineries, Space Installations, and Major Industrial Units.

Key Responsibilities:

Perimeter security, access control, threat assessment, emergency response, coordination with industrial management.

Example: An AC might command security for a nuclear power plant serving millions.

Career Leap

Fast-Track Promotion:

LDCE offers promotion from Non-Gazetted to Group 'A' Gazetted Officer, skipping years of normal seniority-based progression.

Leadership Role:

As AC, you command units, make strategic decisions, and lead teams in critical security operations.

Benefit: Jump 5-7 years in normal promotion timeline.

LDCE Advantage

Internal Competition:

Competition only among serving CISF personnel, not open public candidates.

Field Experience Counts:

Your practical knowledge of CISF operations gives you an edge over fresh candidates.

Opportunity: Best chance for deserving personnel to become officers.

Stage 1: Written Exam

Two Papers held on the same day. Total 400 Marks.

Key Details:

  • Paper I: 300 Marks, 2.5 Hours (Objective)
  • Paper II: 100 Marks, 2 Hours (Descriptive)
  • Negative Marking: 1/3rd for wrong answers in Paper I
  • ‚Ä¢ Weightage: 66.67% of total (400/600)
  • ‚Ä¢ Qualifying marks decided by UPSC

Tip: Time management between Paper I (objective) and Paper II (descriptive) is crucial.

Stage 2: PST/PET

Physical Standard Test & Physical Efficiency Test (Qualifying Only)

  • ‚Ä¢ 100 Meters Race: 16 Seconds (Men), 18 Seconds (Women)
  • ‚Ä¢ 800 Meters Race: 3 Minutes (Men), 4 Minutes (Women)
  • ‚Ä¢ Long Jump: 3.5 Meters (Men), 3 Meters (Women)
  • ‚Ä¢ Shot Put: 4.5 Meters (Men), 3.5 Meters (Women)
  • ‚Ä¢ Includes Medical Standards verification
  • ‚Ä¢ Important: Failure in PET eliminates candidate regardless of written marks

Tip: Start PET practice early while preparing for written exam.

Stage 3: Interview/Personality Test

Personality Test conducted by UPSC board in New Delhi.

  • ‚Ä¢ Marks: 200 Marks.
  • ‚Ä¢ Duration: 30-40 minutes per candidate.
  • ‚Ä¢ Assesses officer-like qualities, leadership, decision making, and professional knowledge.
  • ‚Ä¢ Focus: Current affairs (especially security & industrial), CISF operations, situational responses.
  • ‚Ä¢ Final Merit: Written (400) + Interview (200) = 600 Marks.

Tip: Be updated with recent CISF operations and security challenges.

Complete Syllabus

Paper I (Objective) & Paper II (Descriptive)

Paper I: General Ability & Professional Skill (300 Marks)

  • Part A: General Mental Ability, General Science, Current Affairs, India & World Geography, History of India, Indian Polity & Economy
  • Part B: CISF Act & Rules, Industrial Security, Aviation Security, Disaster Management, VIP Security, Crime & Intelligence, Security of Vital Installations

Paper II: Essay & Comprehension (100 Marks)

  • Essay Writing (Current topics of national/international importance)
  • Precis Writing
  • Comprehension

Professional Skill (Part B) carries 150 marks - your key scoring area.

Aviation Security

Major Responsibility:

CISF provides security at 66+ major airports across India, handling millions of passengers annually.

Key Areas:

Passenger screening, baggage screening, access control, perimeter security, anti-hijacking measures, coordination with airlines and airport authority.

Example: An AC might be posted at Delhi/IGI Airport, Asia's 10th busiest airport.

CISF Officer Career Path

1. Assistant Commandant (AC)

Entry via LDCE. Commands a Company/Unit.
Group A Gazetted Officer. Starting leadership role.

2. Deputy Commandant (DC)

Next promotion (approx 5-6 yrs). Second-in-Command of larger units. Enhanced responsibilities.

3. Commandant

Commands a Battalion / Major Unit. Senior leadership role with significant operational control.

4. Senior Commandant

Commands multiple units or specialized wings. Strategic planning role.

5. DIG -> IG -> ADG -> DG

Top leadership positions. Deputy Inspector General to Director General - highest post in CISF.

Promotions based on performance, departmental exams, and seniority.
