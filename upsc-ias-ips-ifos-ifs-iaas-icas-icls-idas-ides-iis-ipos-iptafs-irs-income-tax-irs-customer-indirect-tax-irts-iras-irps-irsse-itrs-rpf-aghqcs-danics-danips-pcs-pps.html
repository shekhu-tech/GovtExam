All 24 Civil Services

A) All India Services

1. Indian Administrative Service (IAS)

India's premier service. Responsible for policy formulation, implementation, and administration. Roles include SDM, District Magistrate (DM), and Secretaries in Ministries.

2. Indian Police Service (IPS)

Maintains law and order, crime prevention, and detection. Roles include SP, DIG, IGP, and Directors of Intelligence Agencies.

3. Indian Forest Service (IFoS)

Manages country's natural resources, forests, and wildlife. Ensures environmental stability and ecological balance.

B) Group 'A' Civil Services

4. Indian Foreign Service (IFS)

Represents India abroad. Manages diplomatic relations, trade, and cultural ties in Embassies and High Commissions.

5. Indian Audit and Accounts Service (IA&AS)

Audits accounts of Union and State governments and public sector organizations. Works under CAG.

6. Indian Civil Accounts Service (ICAS)

Manages financial administration, payment systems, and accounting for the Federal Government.

7. Indian Corporate Law Service (ICLS)

Regulates corporate sector in India. Ensures compliance with Companies Act.

8. Indian Defence Accounts Service (IDAS)

Manages finances and accounts of the Defence Services (Army, Navy, Air Force) and DRDO.

9. Indian Defence Estates Service (IDES)

Manages defence lands and cantonments across India.

10. Indian Information Service (IIS)

Manages government media wing. Works in PIB, DD, AIR to disseminate information.

11. Indian Postal Service (IPoS)

Runs the India Post network, handles traditional postal services and banking/e-commerce logistics.

12. Indian P&T Accounts and Finance Service (IP&TAFS)

Handles finances for Dept of Posts and Dept of Telecommunications.

13. Indian Railway Protection Force Service (IRPFS)

Ensures security of railway passengers and property.

14. Indian Revenue Service (Customs & Indirect Taxes)

Administers GST, Customs, and Excise duties. Guards economic frontiers.

15. Indian Revenue Service (Income Tax)

Administers Direct Tax laws (Income Tax). Key in revenue collection for the nation.

16. Indian Trade Service (ITS)

Handles India's international trade and commerce. Works under DGFT.

17. Indian Railway Accounts Service (IRAS)

Manages finances and accounts of Indian Railways.

18. Indian Railway Personnel Service (IRPS)

Manages Human Resources (HR) for the massive workforce of Indian Railways.

19. Indian Railway Traffic Service (IRTS)

The operational backbone of Railways. Manages freight and passenger train operations.

C) Group 'B' Civil Services

20. Armed Forces Headquarters Civil Service (AFHQCS)

Provides civilian support staff and administration at Armed Forces HQs.

21. DANICS

Admin services for Delhi, Andaman & Nicobar, Lakshadweep, Daman & Diu, Dadra & Nagar Haveli.

22. DANIPS

Police services for Delhi and Union Territories listed above.

23. Pondicherry Civil Service (PCS)

Administrative service for the UT of Puducherry.

24. Pondicherry Police Service (PPS)

Police service for the UT of Puducherry.

Stage 1: Prelims

Objective Type (MCQ). Qualifying for Mains.

GS Paper I (200 Marks)

100 Questions | 2 Hours | Negative Marking: 1/3rd

  • Current Events: National and International importance.
  • History: Ancient, Medieval, Modern India & Indian National Movement.
  • Geography: Physical, Social, Economic Geography of India & World.
  • Polity: Constitution, Political System, Panchayati Raj, Public Policy, Rights Issues.
  • Economy: Sustainable Dev, Poverty, Inclusion, Demographics, Social Sector Initiatives.
  • Environment: General issues on Ecology, Bio-diversity, Climate Change.
  • General Science: Basic science and technology developments.

CSAT Paper II (200 Marks)

80 Questions | 2 Hours | Negative Marking: 1/3rd

  • Comprehension: Reading and understanding passages.
  • Interpersonal Skills: Including communication skills.
  • Logical Reasoning: Analytical ability.
  • Decision Making: Problem-solving.
  • General Mental Ability: Basic logic.
  • Basic Numeracy: Numbers, orders of magnitude (Class X level).
  • Data Interpretation: Charts, graphs, tables (Class X level).
  • Note: This is Qualifying. You need 33% (66 marks).

Stage 2: Mains

Subjective (Written). 1750 Marks counted for Merit.

Essay (250 Marks)

Candidates may be required to write essays on multiple topics. Tests articulation, thought process, and analytical skills.

GS I: Heritage & Geography (250 Marks)

Indian Heritage and Culture, History of the World & Society, Geography of the World.

GS II: Governance & Polity (250 Marks)

Governance, Constitution, Polity, Social Justice, and International Relations.

GS III: Tech & Economy (250 Marks)

Technology, Economic Development, Bio-diversity, Environment, Security and Disaster Management.

GS IV: Ethics (250 Marks)

Ethics, Integrity, and Aptitude. Case studies on moral dilemmas.

Optional Subject (500 Marks)

Paper VI & Paper VII (250 Marks each). Based on the subject chosen by the candidate.

Qualifying Language Papers

Paper A: Indian Language (300 Marks)
Paper B: English (300 Marks)
Marks not counted for ranking, but mandatory to pass.

Stage 3: Interview

This is the Final Stage (Personality Test). It lasts approx 30-45 minutes.

  • Marks: 275 Marks. Added to Mains score (1750) for final ranking out of 2025.
  • Objective: To assess the candidate's suitability for a career in public service by a Board of competent and unbiased observers.
  • Key Traits Assessed:
    • Mental Alertness
    • Critical Powers of Assimilation
    • Clear and Logical Exposition
    • Balance of Judgement
    • Variety and Depth of Interest
    • Ability for Social Cohesion and Leadership
    • Intellectual and Moral Integrity
  • Location: Dholpur House, UPSC Shahjahan Road, New Delhi.

IAS Career Progression

1. Training (2 Years)

Mussoorie (LBSNAA). Foundation course and Phase-1 training.

2. District Level (4-9 Years)

SDM, District Magistrate (Collector).
Managing law & order, revenue, and development of a district.

3. State Secretariat (10-18 Years)

Director, Joint Secretary. Policy making at state level.

4. Central Secretariat (19+ Years)

Joint Secretary, Additional Secretary to Govt of India.

5. Apex Scale

Chief Secretary (State) or Cabinet Secretary (Highest post in India).
