All 24 Civil Services

A) All India Services

1. Indian Administrative Service (IAS)

Premier service of India. Responsible for policy making, implementation and administration. Roles include SDM, District Magistrate (DM), and Secretary in ministries.

2. Indian Police Service (IPS)

Maintaining law and order, crime prevention and detection. Roles include SP, DIG, IGP, and Directors of intelligence agencies.

3. Indian Forest Service (IFoS)

Manages country's natural resources, forests and wildlife. Ensures environmental sustainability and ecological balance.

B) Group 'A' Civil Services

4. Indian Foreign Service (IFS)

Represents India abroad. Manages diplomatic relations, trade and cultural relations in embassies and high commissions.

5. Indian Audit and Accounts Service (IA&AS)

Audits accounts of central and state governments and public sector organizations. Works under CAG.

6. Indian Civil Accounts Service (ICAS)

Manages financial administration, payment systems and accounting for the federal government.

7. Indian Corporate Law Service (ICLS)

Regulates corporate sector in India. Ensures compliance with Companies Act.

8. Indian Defence Accounts Service (IDAS)

Manages finance and accounts of defence services (Army, Navy, Air Force) and DRDO.

9. Indian Defence Estates Service (IDES)

Manages defence lands and cantonments across India.

10. Indian Information Service (IIS)

Manages government media wing. Works in PIB, DD, AIR to disseminate information.

11. Indian Postal Service (IPoS)

Runs India Post network, handles traditional postal services and banking/e-commerce logistics.

12. Indian Post & Telecommunication Accounts and Finance Service (IP&TAFS)

Handles finance for postal department and telecommunications department.

13. Indian Railway Protection Force Service (IRPFS)

Ensures security of railway passengers and property.

14. Indian Revenue Service (Customs & Indirect Taxes)

Administers GST, customs, and excise duties. Protects economic borders.

15. Indian Revenue Service (Income Tax)

Administers direct tax laws (Income Tax). Crucial for revenue collection for the nation.

16. Indian Trade Service (ITS)

Handles India's international trade and commerce. Works under DGFT.

17. Indian Railway Accounts Service (IRAS)

Manages finance and accounts of Indian Railways.

18. Indian Railway Personnel Service (IRPS)

Manages human resources (HR) for Indian Railways' vast workforce.

19. Indian Railway Traffic Service (IRTS)

Operational backbone of railways. Manages freight and passenger train operations.

C) Group 'B' Civil Services

20. Armed Forces Headquarters Civil Service (AFHQCS)

Provides civilian auxiliary staff and administration in Armed Forces Headquarters.

21. DANICS

Administrative services for Delhi, Andaman & Nicobar, Lakshadweep, Daman & Diu, Dadra & Nagar Haveli.

22. DANIPS

Police services for Delhi and above Union Territories.

23. Puducherry Civil Service (PCS)

Administrative service for Puducherry Union Territory.

24. Puducherry Police Service (PPS)

Police service for Puducherry Union Territory.

Stage 1: Prelims

Objective type (MCQ). Qualifying for Mains.

GS Paper I (200 Marks)

100 Questions | 2 Hours | Negative Marking: 1/3

  • Current Events: National and international importance.
  • History: Ancient, Medieval, Modern India and Indian National Movement.
  • Geography: Physical, Social, Economic Geography of India and World.
  • Polity: Constitution, Political System, Panchayati Raj, Public Policy, Rights Issues.
  • Economy: Sustainable Development, Poverty, Inclusion, Demographics, Social Sector Initiatives.
  • Environment: Ecology, Biodiversity, Climate Change - General issues.
  • General Science: Basic science and technology developments.

CSAT Paper II (200 Marks)

80 Questions | 2 Hours | Negative Marking: 1/3

  • Comprehension: Reading passages and understanding.
  • Interpersonal Skills: Including communication skills.
  • Logical Reasoning: Analytical ability.
  • Decision Making: Problem-solving.
  • General Mental Ability: Basic numeracy.
  • Basic Numeracy: Numbers, order of magnitude (Class X level).
  • Data Interpretation: Charts, graphs, tables (Class X level).
  • Note: This is qualifying. You need 33% (66 marks).

Stage 2: Mains

Subjective (Written). 1750 marks counted for merit.

Essay (250 Marks)

Candidates may be required to write essays on multiple topics. Tests expression, thought process and analytical skills.

GS I: Heritage & Geography (250 Marks)

Indian heritage and culture, history and society of the world, world geography.

GS II: Governance & Polity (250 Marks)

Governance, Constitution, Polity, Social Justice, and International Relations.

GS III: Technology & Economy (250 Marks)

Technology, Economic Development, Biodiversity, Environment, Security and Disaster Management.

GS IV: Ethics (250 Marks)

Ethics, Integrity, and Aptitude. Case studies on moral dilemmas.

Optional Subject (500 Marks)

Paper VI and Paper VII (250 marks each). Based on subject chosen by candidate.

Qualifying Language Papers

Paper A: Indian Language (300 marks)
Paper B: English (300 marks)
Marks not counted for ranking, but passing is mandatory.

Stage 3: Interview

This is the final stage (Personality Test). It lasts about 30-45 minutes.

  • Marks: 275 marks. Added to Mains score (1750) for final ranking, out of total 2025.
  • Purpose: To assess candidate's suitability for career in public service by a board of competent and unbiased observers.
  • Main qualities assessed:
    • Mental alertness
    • Critical powers of assimilation
    • Clear and logical exposition
    • Balance of judgment
    • Variety and depth of interest
    • Social cohesion and leadership ability
    • Intellectual and moral integrity
  • Location: Dholpur House, UPSC Shahjahan Road, New Delhi.

IAS Career Progression

1. Training (2 Years)

Mussoorie (LBSNAA). Foundation Course and Stage-1 Training.

2. District Level (4-9 Years)

SDM, District Magistrate (Collector).
Manage law & order, revenue and development of a district.

3. State Secretariat (10-18 Years)

Director, Joint Secretary. Policy making at state level.

4. Central Secretariat (19+ Years)

Joint Secretary, Additional Secretary Government of India.

5. Top Level

Chief Secretary (State) or Cabinet Secretary (Highest post in India).
