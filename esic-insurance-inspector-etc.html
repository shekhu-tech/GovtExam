5. Mains Exam & Skill Test üìã

The Mains exam is post-specific and tests your in-depth knowledge of the relevant subjects. Some posts also require a computer or skill test.

Mains Exam Structure

The Mains exam for UDC, Assistant, and Inspector posts consists of four sections: Reasoning, General Awareness, Quantitative Aptitude, and English.

The paper has a total of 200 questions to be completed in 2 hours.

Skill Test Requirements

For UDC and Assistant, a Computer Skill Test is conducted (PowerPoint, Word, Excel). For Stenographer, a dictation and transcription test is mandatory.

6. Post-wise Details & Duties üåü

Inspector / Assistant

  • MTS: General office maintenance, and delivery of files.

8. Your Preparation Strategy üìà

Success in the ESIC exam requires a structured and consistent approach. Proper planning and dedicated practice are the keys to cracking the exam.

üìö Key Steps:

  1. Understand the Syllabus

    Thoroughly go through the official syllabus and exam pattern for both Prelims and Mains papers.

  2. Create a Study Plan

    Allocate time for each subject based on your strengths and weaknesses. Focus more on areas where you need improvement.

  3. Practice with Mock Tests

    Regularly take full-length mock tests to get familiar with the exam environment and improve time management.

üîÑ Habits & Practice:

  • Daily Reading: Read newspapers and magazines to stay updated on current affairs and improve English.

  • Revision: Regularly revise what you have studied. This helps in long-term retention of information.

Phase I: Preliminary Exam

This is a common objective-type exam for all posts. It acts as a screening test to shortlist candidates for the Mains. The paper consists of 100 questions from four sections to be completed in 60 minutes. There is a negative marking of 0.25 for each wrong answer.

Phase II: Mains Exam

The Mains exam is specific to the post you have applied for and tests your in-depth knowledge of the relevant subjects. The exam structure varies depending on the category (UDC, Inspector, etc.).

Skill Test & Document Verification

Candidates who qualify in the Mains exam are called for a skill test (where applicable) and document verification. This is a crucial step where your eligibility, as claimed in the application, is verified with original documents.
