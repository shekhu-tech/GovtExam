3. Three Levels of CET ‚úÖ

To cater to different job requirements, the NRA will conduct three separate CETs based on the minimum educational qualification.

  • A **Matriculate (10th Pass) Level** CET for posts like SSC MTS and RRB Group D.
  • A **Higher Secondary (12th Pass) Level** CET for posts like SSC CHSL and RRB NTPC (12th Level).
  • A **Graduate Level** CET for posts like SSC CGL, IBPS PO, and RRB NTPC (Graduate Level).

5. CET Score & Its Validity üèõÔ∏è

The CET score is the most important outcome of the exam, serving as your passport for applying to various government jobs.

1. Score Validity

Your CET score will have a long validity period.

  • The score will be valid for **three years** from the date of result declaration.
  • This allows you to apply for multiple vacancies over the years without retaking the prelims.

2. Multiple Attempts

You can aim to improve your score.

  • There will be no restriction on the number of attempts, subject to the upper age limit.
  • Candidates can appear in the CET to improve their score.
  • The best of all valid scores will be considered the candidate's current score.

6. The Next Stage: Agency-Specific Exams üåü

Clearing the CET is the first step. The final selection depends on the Mains and other stages conducted by the primary recruiting agencies.

SSC (CGL, CHSL etc.)

  • Conducts Tier-II, Tier-III (Descriptive), and Skill Tests.
  • Syllabus is more specialized and in-depth.

IBPS (PO, Clerk etc.)

  • Conducts Mains exam and Interview.
  • Focuses on Banking Awareness, Data Interpretation, and high-level Reasoning.

RRB (NTPC, Group D etc.)

  • Conducts CBT-2, followed by Skill Test or Physical Test.
  • Syllabus is focused on subjects relevant to railway operations.

Stage 1: NRA Common Eligibility Test (CET)

This is the first-level screening for all graduate-level posts. It will be an online exam testing advanced concepts in Reasoning, Quantitative Aptitude, English, and General Awareness. Your score will be valid for three years, allowing you to apply for various Mains exams.

Stage 2: Agency-Specific Mains Exam

Using your valid CET score, you can apply for Mains exams conducted by SSC (for CGL), RRB (for NTPC Graduate posts), and IBPS (for POs). These exams will have a more specialized and in-depth syllabus (e.g., advanced maths in CGL, banking awareness in IBPS PO).

Stage 3: Interview / Final Stage

This is the final hurdle for officer posts. It can include a descriptive paper, a Group Discussion/Exercise, and a Personal Interview. This stage assesses your personality, communication skills, and fitness for an officer role. The final merit is determined by the respective agency.
