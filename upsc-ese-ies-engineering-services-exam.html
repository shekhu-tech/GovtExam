13. Career Roadmap ðŸš€

From AEE to Secretary to Govt of India. The growth is immense with attractive perks.

4 Pillars of ESE

1. Civil Engineering

Infrastructure backbone. Bridges, Roads, Buildings, Water works, Environmental Engineering.

Key Subjects: Structural Analysis, Geotech, Transportation, Environmental

2. Mechanical Engineering

Power, Production, Railways Rolling Stock, Defense machinery, Thermal Systems.

Key Subjects: Thermodynamics, Heat Transfer, Machine Design, Production

3. Electrical Engineering

Power Grids, CPWD Electricals, Defense Electronics, Railways Traction, Control Systems.

Key Subjects: Power Systems, Machines, Control, Measurements

4. Electronics & Telecom

Signaling, Communication, Radio Regulatory, Information Service, Digital Systems.

Key Subjects: Networks, Signals, Digital Electronics, Communication

Stage 1: Prelims

Objective Type (MCQ). 500 Marks total.

Paper I: GS & Aptitude

200 Marks | 2 Hours | 100 Questions

Covers Current Affairs, Engineering Math, Ethics, Design, Energy, Project Management, ICT, Material Science, etc.

Paper II: Technical

300 Marks | 3 Hours | 150 Questions

Core engineering subjects based on your branch.

Note: Calculators are NOT allowed in Prelims. Negative marking 1/3 applies.

Stage 2: Mains

Conventional (Written). 600 Marks total.

Paper I

300 Marks | 3 Hours

Detailed subjective questions from the first half of your technical syllabus.

Paper II

300 Marks | 3 Hours

Detailed subjective questions from the second half of your technical syllabus.

Note: Scientific Calculators are allowed here.

Stage 3: Interview

Personality Test at UPSC Bhawan, New Delhi. Tests engineering aptitude and personality.

  • Marks: 200 Marks.
  • Focus: Technical knowledge, practical applications, leadership potential, communication skills.
  • Panel: UPSC member + Technical Experts from relevant fields.
  • Duration: 30-45 minutes.

GS Paper-I Syllabus

10 Topics Common for All Branches:

  1. Current issues of national and international importance.
  2. Engineering Aptitude (Logical reasoning and Analytical ability).
  3. Engineering Mathematics and Numerical Analysis.
  4. General Principles of Design, Drawing, Importance of Safety.
  5. Standards and Quality practices in production, construction, maintenance.
  6. Basics of Energy and Environment (Pollution, Climate Change).
  7. Basics of Project Management.
  8. Basics of Material Science and Engineering.
  9. Information and Communication Technologies (ICT).
  10. Ethics and Values in Engineering profession.

Career Progression

1. Assistant Executive Engineer (AEE)

Entry Level (Junior Scale). First posting after training.

2. Executive Engineer (EE)

Senior Time Scale (4-5 years). Divisional head level.

3. Superintending Engineer (SE)

JAG/Selection Grade. Regional head.

4. Chief Engineer (CE)

SAG. Zonal head or equivalent.

5. Director General / Member / Chairman

Apex Scale. Head of Department/Service.
