Border Security Force (BSF)

Primary Role:

India's primary border guarding force, defending the borders with Pakistan and Bangladesh. It is responsible for preventing infiltration, smuggling, and military aggression.

Operations:

Riverine operations, desert patrols, border fencing, and anti-smuggling operations.

Example: A BSF AC might lead a company on the Indo-Pak border in Rajasthan or the riverine borders of Bengal.

Central Reserve Police Force (CRPF)

Primary Role:

India's largest Central Armed Police Force, responsible for internal security, counter-insurgency operations (Naxal areas), and maintaining law and order during elections.

Special Units:

COBRA (Commando Battalion for Resolute Action) for anti-Naxal operations, RAF (Rapid Action Force) for riot control.

Example: A CRPF AC could lead a company in the jungles of Chhattisgarh fighting Naxals or in Kashmir managing unrest.

Central Industrial Security Force (CISF)

Primary Role:

Provides security to airports, seaports, nuclear power plants, space establishments, and other critical industrial assets. It is known for its public interface.

Major Installations:

All major airports (Delhi, Mumbai, etc.), Metro systems, vital installations like ONGC refineries.

Example: A CISF AC might manage the security deployment at Delhi International Airport or a Metro station.

Indo-Tibetan Border Police (ITBP)

Primary Role:

Deployed in the high altitudes of the Himalayas on the Indo-China border. They specialize in mountain warfare, skiing, and high-altitude rescue.

Special Training:

Advanced mountaineering, skiing, avalanche rescue, and high-altitude survival techniques.

Example: An ITBP AC could command a border outpost in Ladakh at 15,000 feet.

Sashastra Seema Bal (SSB)

Primary Role:

Guards India's open borders with Nepal and Bhutan. They focus on preventing smuggling and human trafficking while promoting a sense of security among border populations.

Unique Approach:

Combines border security with community engagement and development programs.

Example: An SSB AC might lead a company on the Indo-Nepal border, conducting patrols and community interactions.

Paper I Syllabus (250 Marks)

Objective Type | 125 Questions | Negative Marking

A. General Mental Ability (50 Questions)

  • Logical reasoning, analytical ability
  • Quantitative aptitude (basic numeracy)
  • Data interpretation (charts, graphs, tables)
  • Data sufficiency

B. General Science (25 Questions)

  • Physics, Chemistry, Biology
  • Environmental Science (pollution, climate change)
  • Everyday science applications

C. General Studies (50 Questions)

  • History of India & freedom struggle
  • Indian and World Geography
  • Indian Polity and Constitution
  • Indian Economy and planning
  • Current events of national and international importance

This paper tests speed and accuracy. Time management is crucial.

Paper II Syllabus (200 Marks)

Descriptive Type | English or Hindi (Essay only) | 3 Hours

Part A: Essay (80 Marks)

  • Topics on modern Indian history
  • Geography, polity, economy of India
  • Security and human rights issues
  • Science, technology, and environmental issues
  • Current national and international events

Part B: Comprehension & Writing (120 Marks)

  • Comprehension passages (English only)
  • Precis writing (summary skills)
  • Counter arguments
  • Simple grammar and usage
  • Report writing (structured format)

This paper tests articulation and structured thinking. Practice writing regularly.
