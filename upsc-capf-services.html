Border Security Force (BSF)

The BSF is India's primary border guarding force, defending the borders with Pakistan and Bangladesh. It is responsible for preventing infiltration, smuggling, and military aggression.

Example: A BSF AC might lead a company on the Indo-Pak border in Rajasthan or the riverine borders of Bengal.

Central Reserve Police Force (CRPF)

The CRPF is India's largest Central Armed Police Force, responsible for internal security, counter-insurgency operations (Naxal areas), and maintaining law and order during elections.

Example: A CRPF AC could lead a company in the jungles of Chhattisgarh fighting Naxals or in Kashmir managing unrest.

Central Industrial Security Force (CISF)

The CISF provides security to airports, seaports, nuclear power plants, space establishments, and other critical industrial assets. It is known for its public interface.

Example: A CISF AC might manage the security deployment at Delhi International Airport or a Metro station.

Indo-Tibetan Border Police (ITBP)

The ITBP is deployed in the high altitudes of the Himalayas on the Indo-China border. They specialize in mountain warfare, skiing, and high-altitude rescue.

Example: An ITBP AC could command a border outpost in Ladakh at 15,000 feet.

Sashastra Seema Bal (SSB)

The SSB guards India's open borders with Nepal and Bhutan. They focus on preventing smuggling and human trafficking while promoting a sense of security among border populations.

Example: An SSB AC might lead a company on the Indo-Nepal border, conducting patrols and community interactions.
